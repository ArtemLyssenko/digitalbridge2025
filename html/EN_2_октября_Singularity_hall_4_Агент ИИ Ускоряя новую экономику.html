The Era of AI Agents: from Chatbots to Autonomous Super-Employees

Kai-Fu Lee — CEO of Sinovation Ventures and 01.ai

Annotation

The speaker made the case for the shift from chatbots and tools to autonomous AI agents with reasoning, planning, and memory. They showed the value of multi-agent systems using HR automation as an example and demonstrated creating a travel website for Kazakhstan in 5 minutes. Emphasis was placed on open models and on-prem deployment, as well as partnerships and practical implementation formats for companies without their own AI teams.

Goals

  • Show the strategic shift to autonomous AI agents and their impact on productivity and GDP
  • Demonstrate multi-agent scenarios and practical business value
  • Justify the choice of open models and local deployment for security and flexibility
  • Offer a platform and collaboration formats for companies without AI engineers
  • Define metrics for agent effectiveness through business outcomes rather than replacing people

Key Questions

  • How to measure the effectiveness of AI agents: by economic outcomes or by role substitution?
  • What architecture and feedback are needed for multi-agent systems in real business?
  • Where are the boundaries of agent autonomy and what role remains for humans in the loop?
  • What are the risks and advantages of open models and on-prem deployment for enterprises?
  • How can companies without AI teams quickly start and scale agent implementation?

Key Points

  • Modern models have shifted from prediction to reasoning: mathematical thinking, code, planning, and an analogue of slow thinking.
  • Evolution: chatbots → tools → copilots → autonomous agents with memory and access to applications, devices, and data.
  • Agents as "super-employees": they accept goals, decompose tasks, and execute them end-to-end.
  • Two classes of agents: workflow (process) and thinking (capable of solving new, uncertain tasks).
  • Multi-agent systems make it possible to automate entire functions (example of a full HR loop with five agents and a feedback loop).
  • Agents are scalable, replicable, operate 24/7, are multilingual; the cost of their work will drop by orders of magnitude.
  • Agent effectiveness should be assessed by value created: profitability, growth, efficiency, not by the number of employees replaced.
  • Open models and local deployment provide control over data, transparency, and flexibility (analogies: Linux, Android).
  • Demo: an AI agent created a travel website about Kazakhstan in three languages in 5 minutes, including content and images.
  • For business, three implementation formats are offered: self-service on the platform, co-development, and turnkey project; partnerships with Alio and Kazakhtelecom already exist.

Quotes

Conclusions

  • AI agents are moving into the phase of autonomous task executors and can automate entire business functions with measurable economic impact.
  • For sustainable adoption, open models, on-prem deployment, and a feedback loop that improves agents based on outcomes are critical.
  • Human involvement remains for empathy and oversight, but the operational part is increasingly shifting to multi-agent systems.
  • Partnerships and flexible implementation formats accelerate the digital transformation of companies without their own AI teams.

Decisions Made

  • Launch pilot projects with Kazakh partners (Alio and Kazakhtelecom) to implement AI agents.
  • Provide the platform in three engagement formats: self-service, co-development, and turnkey project.
  • Orient projects toward open models and local deployment to meet security and data sovereignty requirements.
  • Evaluate agents’ contribution by business outcomes (profitability, revenue growth, efficiency), not by staff replacement metrics.

Action Items

  • Select 2–3 priority processes for the pilot (e.g., recruiting/HR) and define target KPIs.
  • Prepare data sources and integrations: corporate databases, LinkedIn, email, calendars, booking services.
  • Define the stack: a base open LLM, on-prem/private cloud infrastructure, access and logging policy.
  • Design a multi-agent architecture and a feedback loop (quality evaluations, learning from outcomes).
  • Run a quick demonstration case (website/content generation in multiple languages) to engage stakeholders.
