Fireside chat: AI in sports: training, efficiency, and the global audience

Sol Campbell — Former England national team and Arsenal player, investor and speaker
Talgat Dosanov — Head of Solana in Kazakhstan, Founder of Intebix

Annotation

Sol Campbell shared elite sports experience and showed how AI democratizes quality training through video analysis and personalized recommendations. They discussed the gap between the number of players and the shortage of coaching attention and how digital tools close this gap 24/7. The business potential of the sports technology market was highlighted with an emphasis on APAC growth. Parallels were drawn between building a strong team in football and in a tech startup.

Goals

  • Show the role of AI in personalizing training and improving the efficiency of athlete preparation
  • Democratize access to elite coaching for youth and professionals regardless of location and resources
  • Discuss how to close the shortage of coaching attention and create continuous learning 24/7
  • Formulate the product vision for Global 360 and its value for coaches and players
  • Identify investment and market opportunities with a focus on the fast-growing APAC region

Key Questions

  • What was missing in elite athlete preparation and how could AI make up for it?
  • How can an AI platform help both professionals and backyard amateurs equally?
  • What are the similarities between running a Premier League club and a technology company when building a team?
  • What are the metrics and market for sports technologies today and by 2040?
  • How do you cultivate discipline, resilience, and love for the craft in youth?

Key Points

  • AI and video analysis provide personalized technique recommendations (passes, shots, vision–movement coordination), closing the coaching attention gap at a 1:20 ratio or more.
  • Learning should be continuous: the platform provides 24/7 access, multi-currency payments and installments to remove barriers to entry.
  • A strong team is key to success both in sports and in a startup: the right roles, diversity of thinking and culture, ethical balance, and challenge for growth.
  • There is a noticeable decline in street football in England; technology helps compensate for the loss of a natural playing environment.
  • Deep mastery of a skill comes through practice, self-reflection, and embracing mistakes as a source of learning.
  • Some teenage athletes live in disadvantaged areas (up to 70% according to the speaker), around 40% are from the lower social strata; digital tools are critical for their access to quality training.
  • The sports technology market is estimated at ~$23 billion today and could grow to ~$140 billion by 2040; APAC is expected to double and become a growth driver.
  • The platform targets both male and female athletes, as well as coaches: a community and a 'handbook' of solutions are being formed based on user cases.
  • The project team includes footballers, IT specialists, kinesiologists, and sports medicine physicians — a multidisciplinary foundation of the product.
  • If such a tool had been available earlier, it could have strengthened the speaker's career and influenced key matches (example: the 2006 Champions League final).

Conclusions

  • AI can scale quality coaching and make up for the shortage of coaches by providing personalized recommendations based on video analytics.
  • Discipline, resilience, and team culture remain the foundation of success for both athletes and technology teams; technology amplifies but does not replace these fundamentals.
  • The sports technology market is growing rapidly, with potential in APAC, which makes such platforms attractive to users and investors.

Decisions Made

  • Use AI-based video analytics as the product core for personalized athlete recommendations.
  • Focus on accessibility: multi-currency payment model and installments to lower barriers to entry.
  • Develop a community and knowledge base ('handbook') from user cases and coaching practices.
  • Build a multidisciplinary team (sports, IT, kinesiology, sports medicine) as a standard for product development.
  • Prioritize scaling in the APAC region given the expected market growth.
