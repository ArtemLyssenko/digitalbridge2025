Strategies in the Central Asian venture ecosystem: why buy startups

Murat Abdrakhmanov — Founder of MA7 Ventures, the largest venture investor in the region
Timur Turlov — Chief Executive Officer of Freedom Holding Corp.
Bagdat Musin — Chairman of the Management Board of JSC «Казахтелеком»
Elzhan Kushekbayev — Venture investor and partner at MA7 Ventures
Gusein Makhmudov — Head of external innovation at PASHA Holding, managing partner at INMerge Ventures
Almas Chukin — Managing partner of the investment company Visor

Annotation

The panel focused on why it is beneficial for strategics to buy startups and how this accelerates ecosystem transformation. A global and regional pivot toward growth in M&A was noted, as well as the example of the Freedom Super App as a model of ecosystem gravity. Казахтелеком disclosed the mechanics of partnerships and readiness to acquire mature solutions, Pasha Holding — corporate incubation and venture clienting. The failures of the venture capital infrastructure in the region and the need for institutional LPs and funds of funds were discussed.

Goals

  • Articulate the role of strategic buyers in the growth of startups and Central Asian ecosystems
  • Show criteria for evaluating and integrating startups (audience, market share, talent, synergies)
  • Discuss the venture capital infrastructure and ways to attract institutional LPs
  • Identify models of cooperation between corporations and startups (bundle sales, venture clienting, accelerators)
  • Define preparatory steps for startups toward successful M&A deals

Key Questions

  • Why is it more advantageous for corporations to buy startups rather than build solutions in-house?
  • How should strategics assess a startup's value: market share, audience LTV, team, or financial dynamics?
  • Which integration approaches allow preserving the founders' culture and teams?
  • What regulatory and institutional barriers hinder the emergence of a full-fledged VC market in Kazakhstan?
  • Can «Казахтелеком» act as a strategic buyer, and on what terms?

Key Points

  • The global M&A market is reviving: in Q1 2025 growth is about 25%, 98 deals worth $100+ billion; regional deals have also picked up.
  • Freedom's strategy: buy mature services with audience and market share (Aviata, PayBox, Ticketon, Arbuz) to increase Super App gravity and cross-sell into high-margin financial services.
  • Ecosystem effect: standalone businesses find it harder to survive; the second player in a niche survives if it becomes part of a strong ecosystem.
  • Talent as the core value: 'people decide everything'—acquiring strong teams and founders justifies high valuations at early stages.
  • Казахтелеком, via Activation Hub, builds industry tracks and bundle sales of startups in B2B/B2G/B2C; examples: AI therapist ASKOL (30 polyclinics), video badges for feldshers.
  • Казахтелеком is ready to consider purchases of mature startups and is already a channel to government customers; the plan is fiber to 3,000 villages and growth of the customer base.
  • Pasha Holding develops corporate innovation (incubation of 20k+ employees, venture clienting, events) and has executed deals (marketplace «Компето», wallet M10).
  • The region lacks a foundation for VC: there are no institutional LPs (pension, banks) due to regulatory restrictions; the market remains a 'statistical margin of error.'
  • MA7: M&A is key to speed and pivoting; startups attract and retain talent more effectively via options, creating products 'cheaper and faster' than corporations.
  • Ecosystem metrics and traffic monetization are critical: an audience is valuable only when converted into high-margin products; trust and taking greater long-term risk matter.

Conclusions

  • An ecosystem strategy and targeted M&A create 'gravity' for customers and teams, accelerating growth and monetization.
  • State and quasi-state sector players can become effective scaling channels for startups through bundle sales and B2G.
  • The VC market needs institutional LPs and regulatory changes to flourish; without them the market volume will remain small.
  • Cross-country partnerships (Kazakhstan–Azerbaijan–Uzbekistan) expand the sales market and increase the chances of sustainable exits.

Decisions Made

  • «Казахтелеком» confirms readiness to consider acquisitions of mature startups and to scale them through existing sales channels (B2B/B2G/B2C).
  • «Казахтелеком» will continue the work of Activation Hub and industry pilots (including ASKOL and video badges) with an eye to replication.
  • Pasha Holding maintains its course on corporate innovation and venture clienting and opens doors for startups from Kazakhstan to the Azerbaijan market.
  • Freedom concentrates efforts on deep integration of acquired services into the Super App and will apply M&A where ecosystem gravity is strengthened.

Action Items

  • Form a working group on 'ecosystem' metrics (retention, interaction frequency, cross-sell) and their regular publication to the market.
  • Initiate a dialogue on regulatory changes to admit institutional LPs (pension funds, insurers) into VC and funds of funds.
  • Prepare an M&A playbook for startups: financial and technical documentation, compliance, HR/ESOP, integration plan, and synergy KPIs.
  • Expand pilots with «Казахтелеком» in eHealth and security (scaling ASKOL, implementing video badges) and prepare bundled offers for B2G.
  • Launch a cross-border funnel (Kazakhstan–Azerbaijan–Uzbekistan): a startup roadshow with the participation of Pasha Holding, Freedom, Kazakhtelecom.
  • Develop a channel for bundle sales of startup services through the infrastructure of «Казахтелеком» for 100k B2B and 2 million B2C customers.
