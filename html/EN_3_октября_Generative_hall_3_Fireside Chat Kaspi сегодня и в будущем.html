Fireside Chat: Kaspi Today and in the Future

Vitaliy Volyanyuk — Media expert and co-founder of Digital Business
Mikhail Lomtadze — Co-founder and Chairman of the Management Board of Kaspi.kz

Annotation

We discussed how Kaspi achieved ~75% population reach and a uniquely broad range of services: a bet on quality, trust, and product discipline in a small market with no 'second chance.' We unpacked the internal product factory: a three-page brief, testing 'on ourselves,' launch in 3–6 months, and continuous NPS cycles. We showed key growth vectors — SMEs via the Kaspi platform and end-to-end application of AI with the human-in-the-loop principle. International expansion confirmed: launching Kaspi services in Turkey based on the country's second-largest e-commerce platform.

Goals

  • Understand the factors behind Kaspi’s durable success and trust in the Kazakhstan market
  • Unpack Kaspi’s process for developing and launching products without 'raw' MVPs
  • Identify areas for further growth: SMEs, new services, and overseas markets
  • Show practices and principles for implementing AI, including ethics and the human role
  • Outline organizational approaches to metrics, quality, and accountability

Key Questions

  • What are the key reasons for Kaspi’s phenomenal success and reach?
  • Why doesn’t Kaspi release 'raw' products, and how is the internal lab organized?
  • Where is the potential for further growth within Kazakhstan given the high penetration?
  • How is responsibility for financial metrics and KPIs distributed in the company?
  • How and where does Kaspi use artificial intelligence, and when will the company become global?

Key Points

  • Service quality and customer trust are the foundation of a multi-product platform; in a small market there is no second chance.
  • Kaspi deliberately does not roll out raw functionality: experiments are run 'on ourselves,' not on customers.
  • A three-page document in plain language is the entry filter for launch: current experience, target experience, FAQ, business requirements.
  • Average service rollout cycle is 3–6 months; thereafter — continuous improvements and NPS measurement every three months.
  • Strong growth focus — SMEs: marketplace, advertising, delivery, machines/kiosks, and technological infrastructure.
  • Most employees are oriented toward product and quality; financial health is controlled by a narrow circle of top management.
  • About 2–2.5 thousand specialists are involved in building and supporting products: engineers, data scientists, designers.
  • AI is applied everywhere in decision-making: cash forecasting for ATMs, delivery route optimization, etc.
  • Human-in-the-loop principle: AI is an assistant; the final decision remains with the user/employee.
  • Expansion: the second-largest e-commerce platform in Turkey has been acquired; groundwork is being laid to launch Kaspi services.

Conclusions

  • A strategy of quality and simplicity is key to scaling a super app in a small market and is the basis of trust.
  • A systematic product process and a data-driven AI approach ensure sustainable growth and expansion into SMEs.
  • Global expansion has started with Turkey; the Kaspi model is being prepared for transfer to other markets.

Decisions Made

  • Confirmed refusal to release 'raw' products and continuation of testing new features on internal users.
  • Maintain the human-in-the-loop principle: AI is an assistant; final decisions are made by humans.
  • Continue scaling services for SMEs via the Kaspi platform (marketplace, delivery, advertising, machines).
  • Ensure transparency in customer interactions: the virtual assistant is always identified as a robot.
  • Accelerate the launch of Kaspi services in Turkey based on the acquired e-commerce platform.

Action Items

  • Complete and launch the Kaspi services platform in Turkey for customers and partners.
  • Prepare and bring to market Kaspi AI tools for customers and SME partners.
  • Expand offerings for SMEs: logistics, advertising, payment automation, and access to Kaspi infrastructure.
  • Continue quarterly cycles of improvements in NPS and quality, strengthening feedback collection and analysis.
  • Strengthen data science and product development teams in Kazakhstan to scale AI solutions.
