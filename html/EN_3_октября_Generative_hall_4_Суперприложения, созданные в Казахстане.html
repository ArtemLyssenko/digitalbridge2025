Super apps built in Kazakhstan

Vitaliy — session moderator, Digital Bridge
Timur Turlov — Chief Executive Officer of Freedom Holding Corp.
Arsen Tomsky — Founder and Chief Executive Officer of inDrive
Mikhail Lomtadze — Co-founder and Chairman of the Management Board of Kaspi.kz

Annotation

Leaders of Freedom, inDrive, and Kaspi discussed how Kazakhstan’s super apps scale audience and revenue and enter new markets. Different strategic approaches were highlighted: proprietary services vs partner integrations, and “Fair Play” as inDrive’s philosophy. Plans for international expansion (Central Asia, the Caucasus, Turkey) and new verticals were voiced — from foodtech and fintech to education and healthcare. A separate emphasis was placed on the role of regulation and ecosystem cooperation.

Goals

  • Define sustainable models for growth and monetization of super apps in Kazakhstan and abroad
  • Compare strategies: owning services vs partner integrations within super apps
  • Discuss priority verticals for scaling (fintech, foodtech, health, education)
  • Identify barriers and opportunities for international expansion considering regulation
  • Show the social and ethical dimension (Fair Play, access to technology, eco projects)

Key Questions

  • How to maintain a high growth pace and company valuation in a small domestic market?
  • How replicable is the Kazakhstani super app model in foreign markets with different regulation?
  • What exactly is “Kazakhstani” in inDrive’s global product and its super app?
  • How to combine proprietary services and partner integrations within one app space?
  • Which next-generation verticals (health, education) will become ecosystem drivers?

Key Points

  • Freedom: ~11 million ecosystem customers; SuperApp MAU 1.6–1.7 million, installs >3 million; VAU is close to MAU, indicating high frequency of use.
  • Freedom’s revenue mix is diversified: ~30% bank, ~30% brokerage, ~30% insurance; rapid customer flow into the SuperApp and 10% m/m metric growth in core banking.
  • Freedom’s international expansion: launching a bank in Tajikistan (setup from scratch), preparing licenses in a number of Central Asia and Caucasus markets; regulation is the main barrier.
  • Freedom thesis: SuperApp is a working universal model; surviving in stand‑alone banking/insurance will become increasingly difficult.
  • inDrive: ~60 million MAU in 48 countries; the largest R&D/operational hub in Kazakhstan (~1,000 employees) builds solutions for the whole world.
  • inDrive Super App strategy — “Fair Play”: entering domains with imbalances in fairness/access (foodtech via dark stores, shopping including «Рядом»; fintech loans to gig_workers at local bank rates in 6 countries, but not in Kazakhstan due to over‑indebtedness).
  • inDrive’s social initiatives from Kazakhstan to the world: «Дом Мамы», EnvisionU (leadership school), anti‑smog projects (Almaty; considering Lahore/Kathmandu).
  • Kaspi: develops the super app through customer demand and partnerships; integrated Glovo (after an unsuccessful experience with Chocofood), with an emphasis on NPS and operational quality.
  • Kaspi strengthens its position in Turkey (Hepsiburada is the second‑largest marketplace by traffic; approval of the purchase of a small bank for financial services), with a focus on KZ–TR cross‑border trade and fast deliveries.
  • The next wave of verticals: health and education — digital clinics, AI assistants for patients and doctors, EdTech partnerships (e.g., with Bilim Media), individualized learning paths and anti‑cheating.

Conclusions

  • Kazakhstani super apps are moving from product bundles to platforms with social value and international scale.
  • Hybrid models (owning services + partner integrations) increase the speed and quality of the offering, while the customer’s voice remains decisive.
  • Regulation is a key factor in expansion; successful adaptation requires localization, but the core of the customer experience can be ported.
  • New verticals (health, education) and AI tools will become the next growth levers for ecosystems.

Decisions Made

  • Kaspi scales the Glovo integration inside the app given positive user feedback.
  • Freedom finalizes the bank launch in Tajikistan and continues obtaining licenses in the Central Asia and Caucasus markets, replicating the superapp model.
  • inDrive ramps up the foodtech direction through dark stores and M&A (including «Рядом») and expands fintech lending to gig_workers in international locations (without launching in Kazakhstan).
  • Kaspi and Hepsiburada accelerate cross‑border trade between Kazakhstan and Turkey with a focus on delivery speed.
  • Ecosystems strengthen cooperation with technology partners (example: Kaspi–Яндекс in express delivery).

Action Items

  • Freedom: finalize regulatory approvals and the bank’s go‑live in Tajikistan; prepare a phased release of products (bank, brokerage, insurance) and customer onboarding into the SuperApp.
  • Freedom: launch pilots in the health and education verticals (digital clinic, AI assistants, school diary/kids’ cards with Bilim Media) and success metrics (MAU/retention/NPS).
  • Kaspi: run a wave of communications and promo for the Glovo integration, implement an improvement cycle based on NPS/CSAT, and ensure SLA jointly with the partner.
  • Kaspi: advance obtaining a banking license in Turkey and prepare a package of fintech products for Hepsiburada, including solutions for cross‑border payments and seller financing.
  • inDrive: scale «Рядом» and dark stores to new districts and cities; standardize operational KPIs (assortment, freshness, 15‑minute delivery).
  • inDrive: expand the geography of lending to gig_workers at bank rates and strengthen scoring based on platform data.
  • inDrive: determine the next wave of cities for anti‑smog initiatives (evaluating Lahore/Kathmandu), form partnerships with municipalities and NGOs.
  • For regulators: simplify and make fintech/bank licensing more transparent to enable the expansion of Kazakhstani ecosystems, and implement sandbox mechanisms for pilots.
