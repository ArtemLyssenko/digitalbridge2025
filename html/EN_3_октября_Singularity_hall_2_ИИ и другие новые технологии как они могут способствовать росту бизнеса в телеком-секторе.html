AI and other new technologies: how they can drive business growth in the telecom sector

Abylai Dzhumageldinov — Director of Marketing and Development for the Corporate Business Division at JSC 'Казахтелеком'
Bayan Konirbaev — Deputy Chair of the Board of Trustees, alem.ai
Nabil Ben Sussia — Chief Commercial Officer, IEC Telecom Group (Group CCO)
Farrukh Farajullaev — Chief Commercial Officer, AzInTelecom
Tatyana Sayfarova — Founder of Казахстанская ассоциация экспертов клиентского сервиса, Independent Consultant to JSC 'Казахтелеком'

Annotation

The panel discussed how AI and satellite/edge technologies are creating new growth in telecom. Operators are balancing between monetizing AI services and selling infrastructure (GPU‑as‑a‑Service). Edge architecture and multi‑clouds are changing network orchestration, while satellites (LEO, Starlink, D2D) are narrowing the digital divide. Customer experience is being rebuilt on AI analytics: noticeable optimization has already been achieved (−22% repeat calls) with data security and sovereignty prioritized.

Goals

  • Understand how AI, edge, and satellite technologies enhance growth and operational efficiency of the telecom business
  • Define the balance between commercializing AI services and developing infrastructure (GPU services, edge data centers)
  • Formulate an approach to AI security and data sovereignty when scaling digital services
  • Discuss the convergence of satellite and terrestrial networks (including D2D) for coverage of remote areas
  • Outline a plan for transforming customer service based on AI analytics and assistants

Key Questions

  • How should telecom operators balance their strategy: sell AI services or the infrastructure for AI?
  • What edge/AI‑native architecture is needed for new SLAs and low latency in 5G/IoT scenarios?
  • What key risks do AI technologies pose, and how to build legal and technical safeguards?
  • How will the convergence of satellite LEO networks and D2D affect interaction with mobile operators?
  • What metrics and effects have already been confirmed in customer service when using AI?

Key Points

  • Казахтелеком is developing a dual strategy: GPU‑as‑a‑Service for the market and joint AI products with startups; in parallel, it is implementing AI in internal processes (call center, ticket automation, network optimization).
  • AI video analytics is in demand primarily in education and smart infrastructure; customers are already allocating budgets for such projects.
  • Edge architecture is becoming critical: micro data centers closer to data sources provide SLAs and low latency for new services.
  • Orchestrating distributed compute and the network (NSM) requires new methodology and competencies; telecom is becoming a platform for services, not just 'connectivity'.
  • Multi‑cloud with a balance of Chinese and Western providers (AWS, Azure, Tencent, JD, Alibaba) is a practical path for the region.
  • The convergence of satellite and terrestrial networks has accelerated with the arrival of Starlink: mass market, reduced digital inequality, and access to new applications beyond cities.
  • D2D in its current form is limited: basic messaging is possible with delays and low signal levels; mass telephony 'phone to satellite' is still technologically constrained.
  • In customer service, AI has already delivered impact: 100% analysis of inquiries, orders of magnitude faster, −22% repeat calls; AI assistants will be deployed after refining the knowledge base.
  • AI risks are growing (AI‑generated cyberattacks, ethical dilemmas); AI‑for‑AI defenses, legal frameworks, education, and operational 'kill switches' are needed.
  • Data sovereignty and security are a priority: personal data remain on‑prem within the company/country perimeter; the principles of data minimization and algorithm transparency are applied.

Conclusions

  • AI and edge infrastructure are becoming the core of the new telecom model: operators are evolving into platforms with distributed computing and multi‑clouds.
  • Satellite technologies (LEO, D2D) do not compete but complement terrestrial networks, accelerating the connection of remote areas and reducing the digital divide.
  • Customer service is the pioneer of AI’s practical value: analytics on 100% of inquiries and measurable impact on NRR/repeat contacts while strictly ensuring data sovereignty.

Decisions Made

  • Казахтелеком will scale GPU‑as‑a‑Service and continue co‑developing AI solutions with startups for commercialization.
  • The Казахтелеком contact center is moving to deploy an AI assistant after completing a validated knowledge base and further model training.
  • Alim AI, together with operators, will prepare a methodology and a 3–5‑year roadmap for AI‑native/edge architecture and compute orchestration.
  • AS Telecom, together with mobile operators, will work out integration models for satellite (LEO, D2D) and terrestrial networks to cover hard‑to‑reach regions.
  • Create an inter‑industry working group on AI security (detection/response to AI cyber threats, legal and ethical standards).

Action Items

  • Launch AI video analytics pilots in schools and Smart City projects with measurable SLAs (latency, availability).
  • Design a network of micro data centers at data sources (edge) and update transport network requirements for new IoT/5G loads.
  • Conduct a personal data audit and set up on‑prem storage/processing with transparent algorithms and the principle of data minimization.
  • Organize a reskilling program: CVM/analytics, MLOps/NetDevOps, NSM orchestration, and AI security.
  • With mobile operators and satellite providers, test D2D scenarios (emergency SMS/SOS in mountains and remote areas).
  • Implement AI‑for‑AI cyber defense tools in the SOC (behavioral analytics, front detection, automated response).
