AI, Big Tech & Banking: the transformation of the financial landscape

Aleksandr Shenko — moderator; WU Pay, Terricon Valley
Askar Namazbaev — Director, Deputy Head of the EBRD in Kazakhstan
Usen Galym — Deputy Chairman of the Management Board, Baiterek National Managing Holding JSC
Vyacheslav Kim — Executive Director, Freedom Bank
Dulatbek Ikbaev — Managing Partner, McKinsey & Company in Central Asia
Daniyar Uspanov — Founder and CEO, Tayyab

Annotation

The panel discussed practical cases of AI deployment in banking and the public sector, the role of development institutions in digitalization and infrastructure, and Kazakhstan’s strategy in the era of AI agents. The EBRD presented support areas (startups, cybersecurity, digital cities, green infrastructure). Baiterek showed how an LLM helps standardize access to 70+ financial instruments and speeds up hiring. Freedom demonstrated an MVP AI assistant in the SuperApp with real transactions. McKinsey emphasized the shift from “bots” to AI agents and the need for a total mindset change.

Goals

  • Define AI development priorities in Kazakhstan’s financial sector: human capital and infrastructure
  • Show real cases of deploying AI/AI agents in banks and quasi-state institutions
  • Shape approaches to scaling AI with an emphasis on security and cyber-resilience
  • Clarify the role of development institutions (EBRD, Baiterek) in supporting startups and digital projects
  • Outline the framework for BigTech–startup collaborations and Islamic fintech initiatives

Key Questions

  • Which EBRD digitalization and AI projects are most illustrative for Kazakhstan’s financial sector, and what are the next steps for infrastructure and human capital?
  • How is the Baiterek holding simplifying access to financial services and using AI for customer and HR processes?
  • How is AI helping Freedom Bank speed up processes (compliance, fraud, scoring, support) and what will the launch of the AI assistant change?
  • What limitations and risks does McKinsey see in Kazakhstan’s transition to AI, and where are the bottlenecks (data, culture, architecture)?
  • How does Islamic fintech combine AI with Sharia norms, and where are the best application areas (blockchain, smart contracts)?

Key Points

  • EBRD — the largest foreign investor outside oil and gas (>€10 billion) and an active driver of digitalization: healthcare (HIS and cybersecurity), transport, energy, municipalities, Cities Digital Initiative, cybersecurity of critical infrastructure (incl. KEGOC).
  • EBRD support for startups: the Star Venture program in Kazakhstan (with a new cohort intake at the end of October), mentoring and market access.
  • EBRD focus on infrastructure for the climate transition and low-cost “green” energy as a base for data centers and AI workloads.
  • Baiterek: assets ~KZT 14 trillion, 9 financial organizations, >70 instruments; a single web portal and a virtual assistant based on Anthropic’s LLM (AWS infrastructure) help entrepreneurs find the right products.
  • AI in Baiterek’s HR: AI ranking of resumes by vacancy requirements reduces the load on recruiters under a staffing freeze.
  • Freedom SuperApp: 3.2 million MAU in 1.5 years; AI embedded in information security, fraud, scoring, compliance, support; demonstration of an MVP AI assistant with a real transfer in live operation.
  • Transition from chatbots to AI agents: assistants as an alternative app interface; integration of LLMs (GPT, Gemini) and image/video models (Higgs Field).
  • McKinsey: AI agents are a qualitative leap (hands/eyes for LLMs), dramatically accelerate coding and IT modernization, enable countries and companies to leapfrog, but the main barrier is culture and mindset.
  • Public sector: AI agents can radically cut bureaucracy and corruption risks by automating routine intellectual work; possible optimization of the state apparatus with reinvestment into talent.
  • Islamic fintech: AI does not contradict Sharia with oversight by a Sharia Board; priority is blockchain/smart contracts for the securities market; primary growth focus is Kazakhstan while preparing for expansion.

Conclusions

  • AI in Kazakhstan is moving from pilots to production AI agents in fintech, with mindset leadership and cultural change being critical.
  • Infrastructure (energy, data centers, cybersecurity) and talent development are key scaling factors where the EBRD and Baiterek act as enablers.
  • Competition and BigTech–startup collaborations accelerate innovation; Islamic fintech confirms the compatibility of AI with Sharia norms given proper verification.

Decisions Made

  • Launch of a new cohort intake for the EBRD Star Venture program at the end of October confirmed.
  • Focus institutional support on “green” infrastructure and energy efficiency for data centers and AI workloads.
  • Freedom SuperApp to roll out the MVP AI assistant to production within 2–3 months.
  • Baiterek to expand the use of LLMs in customer and HR processes (virtual assistant, resume ranking) based on AWS and Anthropic models.

Action Items

  • Companies to start with “mindset first”: allocate personal time of top managers (at least 20%) to reskilling and ubiquitous use of AI tools.
  • Conduct a digital maturity and data audit, identify 3–5 priority AI agent use cases (compliance, fraud, support, coding).
  • Prepare an infrastructure roadmap: compute needs, DCs, cyber redundancy, energy efficiency requirements.
  • Startups to apply to the new wave of the EBRD Star Venture; corporations to launch joint pilots with startups (win-win).
  • Public sector to initiate AI agent pilots to automate routine procedures and reduce corruption risks.
  • Baiterek and banks to expand unified portals and AI assistants for product navigation and faster SME onboarding.
