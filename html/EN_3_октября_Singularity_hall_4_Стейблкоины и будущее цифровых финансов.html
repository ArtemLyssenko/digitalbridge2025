Stablecoins and the Future of Digital Finance

Aidana Kaskyrbek — Chief Executive Officer, BAITC
Pavel Khristolyubov — Chief Operating Officer, TOP Wallet
Kyrylo Khomyakov — Regional Head of CEE in Central Asia and Africa, Binance
Tamar Menteshashvili — Head of Stablecoins at Solana Foundation

Annotation

The panel focused on practical stablecoin use cases and Kazakhstan's role as an early driver through a regulatory sandbox and tenge pilots. They discussed key conditions for success: liquidity, distribution, low fees, compliance, and the possible distribution of yield to users. They examined the interaction of stablecoins with CBDC (the digital tenge) and arrived at a complementary role. Infrastructure readiness was noted: cards (Mastercard), QR, wallets, and on/off-ramps for moving from B2B and cross-border payments to retail.

Goals

  • Identify priority stablecoin use cases for Kazakhstan (B2B, cross-border payments, retail via cards and QR).
  • Form requirements for liquidity, listings, and distribution of local (KZT) stablecoins.
  • Build a coordinated regulatory framework and licensing of issuers in the National Bank's sandbox.
  • Reduce user barriers (fees, UX) and provide banking on/off-ramps.
  • Launch pilots with cards and merchant acquiring for mass adoption.

Key Questions

  • What are the real stablecoin use cases in the next 12–24 months, and what will be the first driver of mass adoption?
  • What role do stablecoins play in institutional adoption of crypto, and what barriers hinder their use?
  • Will CBDCs (central bank digital currencies) compete with stablecoins or complement them?
  • Why is Solana becoming an attractive infrastructure for stablecoin issuers, and what is the regulatory treatment?
  • How will competition between local KZT stablecoins and global players (USDT/USDC) evolve given the limited market size?

Key Points

  • Kazakhstan is developing a sandbox for stablecoins and preparing separate licensing; the policy toward tenge-denominated stablecoins is supported by the regulator.
  • The success of a stablecoin is determined by a triad: liquidity and listings; simple buy/sell through banks and low fees; and a clear business incentive (including possible distribution of yield to holders).
  • B2B and cross-border payments are the nearest workable use cases; retail payments via cards (Mastercard), QR, and wallets are the next stage.
  • USDT dominates thanks to global liquidity and distribution; local issuers will have to compete through strategic advantages and integration.
  • Solana attracts issuers with a combination of low costs, high throughput, a broad ecosystem, and features that account for regulatory requirements.
  • The key user barrier is fees and the complexity of on-chain processes; the task of wallets is to 'hide' the complexity and make the UX as familiar as possible.
  • CBDCs and private stablecoins are more likely to complement each other; scaling CBDCs encounters infrastructural and user limitations.
  • For listing pairs on exchanges, volumes and risk management are important (for example, minimum daily turnover and market maker support).
  • Partnerships with global networks and issuers (Mastercard, Circle) save time and costs, accelerating settlements in USDC.
  • Local use cases (e.g., pilots in Alatau City) can become measurable testbeds for mass deployment of stablecoins within the country.

Conclusions

  • Mass adoption of stablecoins in Kazakhstan is more likely to start with B2B and cross-border settlements, then move into retail via cards and QR.
  • Regulatory clarity, liquidity, and distribution are the key to the success of local KZT stablecoins in competing with global issuances.
  • CBDCs and private stablecoins will coexist, but in the short term it is private stablecoins that are showing more practical use cases.

Decisions Made

  • Focus local stablecoin pilots on B2B and cross-border settlements, with a phased rollout into retail payments.
  • Develop liquidity and distribution requirements for listing KZT stablecoins on exchanges (including minimum turnover and market-making).
  • Expand on/off-ramps and card solutions: the launch of non-custodial cards with top-ups in USDC (via Mastercard) has been announced.
  • Continue work in the National Bank's regulatory sandbox and prepare proposals for licensing stablecoin issuers.

Action Items

  • For the regulator: prepare a draft of licensing and supervision rules for stablecoin issuers (including reserve and reporting requirements).
  • For exchanges and issuers: create market-making programs and target liquidity thresholds for pairs with KZT stablecoins.
  • For banks and fintechs: deploy convenient on/off-ramps, integrate stablecoin payments with the national QR and cards.
  • For wallets: hide on-chain complexity and optimize fees, delivering a UX 'like a messenger payment'.
  • For the industry: launch pilots in merchant environments (Alatau City/Astana) with measurable KPIs for volume and payment recurrence.
  • For issuers: test models for distributing yield to holders to incentivize retention and usage.
