Digital Transformation of Corporations

Annotation

The panel discussed how large corporations and the state build responsible AI adoption: from data and API openness to security and audit frameworks. ТШО showcased practical cases of applying Data Science and AI in production, logistics, and workplace safety with a human-in-the-loop approach. Kascode presented the path to creating a Kazakhstani LLM through partnerships with universities and regulators and its open distribution. Plug and Play emphasized ROI-oriented work with startups and a culture of 'experimenting and learning from failures'.

Goals

  • Define frameworks and policies for responsible AI use and data access for large corporations and government agencies
  • Show real corporate cases of modernizing data infrastructure and scaling analytical solutions
  • Identify barriers and conditions for scaling LLM and AI pilots, especially for low-resource languages
  • Form partnership models between the government, corporations, universities, and startups
  • Focus innovation on measurable ROI and operational safety

Key Questions

  • What regulatory frameworks are needed for responsible AI implementation and opening government APIs?
  • How to modernize corporate data infrastructure for large-scale application of Data Science and AI?
  • What are the key challenges for teams building LLMs for low-resource languages, and how can they be scaled?
  • How to build an effective bridge between startups and corporations and filter out the 'noise' at the intake?
  • How to implement a culture of experimentation and manage risks so that failures become a source of knowledge rather than barriers?

Key Points

  • Openness of data and government APIs is a critical condition for accelerating digital transformation; the worst-case scenario is agency-level 'locking' of data and excessive regulation.
  • ТШО applies AI in operational optimization: maintenance forecasting, production planning, workplace safety, supply chain optimization, drone-based computer vision, and corporate chatbots (HR and engineering).
  • The human-in-the-loop approach and strict data governance practices (privacy, authentication, audit, data quality, and logging) are mandatory when scaling AI.
  • Modernization of data infrastructure includes cloud resources, centralized data collection/cleansing, and 'digital champions' as a bridge between domain departments and IT.
  • Kascode is creating a Kazakhstani LLM through a unique partnership (universities, Astana Hub, ministry), making it open-source and multinational in application.
  • The main barriers to AI products are not talent, but regulation and access to quality data; no organization can cope alone.
  • Plug and Play builds ROI-oriented open innovation: a pilot funnel, corporate readiness validation, and support for regional integration in Central Asia.
  • A fail-tolerant culture accelerates innovation: pilots without commercialization also create knowledge and lay the groundwork for subsequent successful launches.
  • It is important for corporations to first set up internal innovation governance (strategy, pilot sandboxes, processes) and only then approach startups.
  • Central Asia can act as a united front in AI scenarios, sharing developments and scaling best practices across countries.

Conclusions

  • Open data and cross-sector partnerships are key to scaling AI in the corporate and public sectors.
  • Security, privacy, and data audit must be built into AI solution architecture from day one.
  • A culture of experimentation and systematic ROI validation through pilots accelerates the path from idea to deployment.

Decisions Made

  • Launch a joint working group at Astana Hub on the opening and standardization of government APIs and data access for AI pilots.
  • Scale the practice of 'digital champions' at ТШО and expand the line of chatbots to new functions and departments.
  • Support further open-source release and localization of the Kazakhstani LLM Kascode and organize pilots with large corporations and government agencies.
  • Organize a regional Plug and Play program in Central Asia with startup selection and corporate validation of solution readiness.

Action Items

  • Hold a roundtable on responsible AI (regulators, corporations, universities, investors) and prepare a set of principles and application checklists.
  • Perform an audit of data quality, logging, and access in critical processes of ТШО; approve an improvement plan and audit frequency.
  • Publish a 'recipe book' for launching LLM projects for low-resource languages based on the Kazakhstani and Catalan cases.
  • Create an inter-corporate pilot sandbox with anonymized datasets through Astana Hub to accelerate PoC and screen out the 'noise'.
  • Form a calendar of hackathons, corporate demo days, and Plug and Play investor sessions in Astana/Almaty with the participation of 5+ corporations.
