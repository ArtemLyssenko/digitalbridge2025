From the first pitch to the first check: the journey of the founder and the investor

Aidana Bergazdenova — Chief Executive Officer, Astana Hub Ventures
Adilbek Kamiev — Managing Director, EA Ventures
Sanzhar Myrzagalym — Founder and Chief Executive Officer, DefectAI
Gulnur Kalimuldina — Founder and Chief Executive Officer, Mirai Tech
Ruslan Rakymbai — Investment Director, Quest Ventures
Baurzhan Aitylbek — Co-founder, Acceleres

Annotation

The panel discussed how founders can get the first check: from tactics of constant networking and regular updates to choosing the timing of the round and selecting the “right” investors with ethics and smart value-add. Investors emphasized the priority of the team over the idea and the importance of in-person communication to assess motivation. Markets were compared: in Central Asia there is less capital and stricter requirements; in the EU/US there is more choice and competition. A separate case of hardware/scientific projects was considered: protection due to a technological barrier and the need for fast venture investments to scale.

Goals

  • Show working strategies for obtaining the first angel and pre-seed checks
  • Align investor and founder expectations at early stages
  • Outline the role of accelerators, grants, and standard documentation (SAFE) in the ecosystem
  • Emphasize the importance of ethics and smart money when choosing an investor
  • Motivate startups from Central Asia to expand to global markets

Key Questions

  • What strategies and tactics are effective for attracting the first check?
  • When should a startup start fundraising: before or after the first customers/metrics?
  • Is venture capital suitable for scientific and hardware projects in Central Asia?
  • How do approaches to early-stage investments differ between Europe/Asia and Central Asia?
  • What criteria do investors use when selecting at pre-seed/seed, and how is it best to approach them?

Key Points

  • Consistency in communication: regular updates (newsletters) and multichannel touchpoints increase the chance of a check.
  • Due diligence on the investor is just as important: check ethics, past investments, and industry relevance.
  • Fundraising timing is individual: many start with bootstrapping and first customers, then bring in venture due to technological shifts (AI, etc.).
  • Accelerators and grants are the optimal path at pre-seed in Central Asia; angel money should be taken selectively.
  • For investors, the key factor is the team: expertise, cohesion, and the “fire in the eyes” matter more than early metrics.
  • In-person communication helps an investor assess a founder’s motivation and values, since the partnership can be long-term.
  • Regional differences: in the EU/US there is more capital and competition for deals; in Central Asia there are fewer funds, therefore the requirements for startups are higher.
  • Hardware/scientific projects have a technological barrier and protection from fast clones, but require rapid investments to scale.
  • Targeted globalization: partnerships and funds orient the portfolio toward international markets, with a focus on fintech, e-commerce, and adjacent niches.
  • Developing content and practices in the Kazakh language strengthens the ecosystem and engages new participants.

Conclusions

  • The first check is about trust and long-term partnership, not just about money.
  • The team and founders' personal motivation are the main criteria for investors at early stages.
  • In Central Asia, it is more effective to start with accelerators/grants and build systematic communication with investors.

Decisions Made

  • Agreed to emphasize evaluation of the team and motivation as the main selection criterion at pre-seed.
  • Recommended that early-stage startups go through accelerators and grant programs before angel checks.
  • Agreed to implement regular newsletters/updates as the standard for founder–investor interaction.
  • Supported the development and use of standard documentation (SAFE at AIFC) for transparent deals.

Action Items

  • Create and maintain a monthly newsletter for current and potential investors with metrics and progress.
  • Develop a checklist for ethical and strategic vetting of investors (cases, reputation, smart money).
  • Apply to relevant accelerators (Astana Hub, international programs) and for equity grants.
  • Organize a workshop on early deals and term sheets based on the book Venture Deals and AIFC SAFE.
  • Set up systematic networking: collect business cards, weekly intros, follow-ups by e-mail and at industry events.
