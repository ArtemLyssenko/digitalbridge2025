From the First Checks to Building a Venture Ecosystem

Murat Abdrakhmanov — Founder of MA7 Ventures, the largest venture investor in the region

Annotation

The speaker showed the evolution of Kazakhstan's venture market: from copy-paste models to global products and teams. Emphasized the key role of angel investments amid an almost complete absence of government money, presented portfolio metrics (TVPI 3.3, DPI 2) and growth cases (Higgsfield, etc.). Separate emphasis: closing the $2–5M round gap through angel syndications and engagement with a fund of funds.

Goals

  • Show the role and metrics of angel investments in the Kazakhstani ecosystem
  • Demonstrate portfolio results and successful exit/growth cases
  • Attract private capital through the fund and MSA Angel Club to close $1–5M rounds
  • Encourage founders to build global products instead of copying
  • Establish interaction with the new fund of funds to scale financing

Key Questions

  • How to ensure a stable inflow of private and institutional capital of at least $100M per year?
  • How to close the late-seed/Series A check gap in the $2–5M range domestically?
  • How to encourage founders and top managers to become business angels (including increasing the share of women)?
  • How to avoid early sales of promising companies due to the lack of local funds for follow-on investments?
  • How to accelerate Kazakhstani startups' entry into global markets from day one?

Key Points

  • The share of angel investments is decreasing in percentage terms but growing in absolute terms; angels dominate at early stages
  • Kazakhstan's venture market volume last year was $76M, with virtually no government participation; private money prevails
  • Over 12 months, 11 startups were financed via the fund and MSA Angel Club for >$8M; with the speaker's co-investments — about $12M (~15% of the Central Asia market)
  • Portfolio metrics: TVPI 3.3 (~$90M on $27M of investments), DPI 2, ARR ~14%; Higgsfield provides a significant contribution to growth
  • Exits: Choco Family sold to Freedom (partially inDrive), HR Messenger — Avito; a number of other successful deals
  • The market has moved past the copy-paste stage: a new generation of founders has emerged, immediately oriented toward global markets
  • Kazakhstani and regional world-class projects: Higgsfield, Educate Online, Influe2, Zealy, Zimran (Finello), DNA Payments, Cody Play, Remofest, Generation Seven, Globers Capital, «Компьютерный конвейер»
  • The vertical small LLM Nace.ai for financial audit shows better latency and fewer hallucinations, works on-prem; in the round — General Catalyst
  • A $2–5M round shortage persists; the market needs at least $100M annually; support from the new fund of funds is expected
  • The club's latest syndication: $3.7M into Higgsfield; in two months, paper return around x4

Quotes

Conclusions

  • Kazakhstan's venture ecosystem has made a qualitative leap and is already producing world-class projects
  • Angel capital remains a critical driver of early stages, but institutional capital is required for scaling
  • The combination of the fund and the angel club has proven effective for closing large rounds and accelerating the growth of portfolio companies

Decisions Made

  • Scale MSA Angel Club as a syndication tool for $1–5M rounds
  • Continue proactively financing globally oriented projects from Kazakhstan and the region
  • Focus efforts on closing the late-seed/Series A gap through syndications and the fund itself
  • Build cooperation with the new fund of funds to attract institutional capital to the market

Action Items

  • Expand the angel club's member base and hold regular pitch sessions, increasing the participation of women investors
  • Form a pool of syndicated checks in the $2–5M range for late-seed/Series A rounds
  • Organize interaction with the fund of funds team (initiative of Oskar Belizbek) and prepare a co-investment pipeline by the end of the year
  • Support portfolio companies in entering MENA, EU, and US markets through partnerships and local expertise
