Content created by AI: film, television, music

Oleksandr Petrenko — Co-founder and CEO of Zibra AI
David Jank — Founding Partner of American Chance
Olzhas Baimagambetov — Head of VK Partner Service
Aisultan Seitov — Founder of QARA Studios

Annotation

The panel discussed whether AI is a co-author or a tool, and how it is changing the production of film, TV, and music. Speakers agreed that AI democratizes entry into the industry and strengthens post- and marketing processes, while final creative decisions remain with humans. Special focus was placed on Kazakhstan’s opportunities: rapid regulation, cultural datasets, and infrastructure can make the country a regional AI hub. The discussion covered the impact of short formats and social media, legal and ethical risks, as well as infrastructure requirements (5G, GPU/NPU) and development horizons (3D, immersive AI, hologram artists).

Goals

  • Articulate the role of AI in film, TV, and music production: co-author vs tool
  • Define priorities for Kazakhstan’s ecosystem (law, data culture, infrastructure) to scale AI content
  • Assess the impact of social networks and short formats on promotion and monetization of content
  • Identify key legal and ethical challenges (copyright, labeling, fair compensation)
  • Outline technological directions for 2–10 years (3D generation, interactive TV, real-time generation)

Key Questions

  • Is AI our co-creator or just a tool in the workflow?
  • When will truly original AI content appear, rather than a recombination of training data?
  • How do geography and cultural specifics affect model training and the adoption of AI in production?
  • Are networks and compute (5G, GPU/NPU) ready for streaming and real-time AI generation?
  • How are TikTok/Instagram/VK platforms evolving, and what is the role of short formats for AI content?

Key Points

  • Democratization: AI lowers the barrier to entry, allowing creators without expensive equipment to showcase their vision and prototype.
  • In film, AI is strongest in script work and post-production; the director’s role as the final decision-maker remains human.
  • Music: AI has become an essential tool for promo and production; music videos yield to the effectiveness of multiple short clips/reels.
  • The “AI-generated” label is gradually losing meaning for audiences: content is perceived as entertainment regardless of how it was created.
  • Geography matters for cultural relevance: local teams and datasets are needed (language, taboos, instruments, voices).
  • Kazakhstan has a window of opportunity: swift regulation, legal protection for authors, registries of voices and instruments, training of lawyers.
  • Legal risks will intensify: major labels and studios will litigate over rights, as has already happened in the digital era.
  • Infrastructure is a critical factor for real-time AI: 5G/network, local GPU/NPU, and enabling policies.
  • Near future: growth of 3D generation and immersive/interactive AI (including TV), the emergence of hologram artists and AI performers.
  • As AI technologies are released faster, the human idea and creative will become the main differentiator and source of trust.

Conclusions

  • AI has already become an integral element of production and promo, augmenting creators and accelerating production, but final creative responsibility remains with the human.
  • Kazakhstan can quickly take the position of the region’s AI hub provided clear rules on copyright, cultural datasets, and infrastructure development are created.
  • Short formats and social platforms are becoming the main distribution funnel, and the boundaries between AI and non-AI content are blurring for the consumer.

Decisions Made

  • Recognize AI as a creator’s companion in a range of tasks (variability, post-production, marketing) while keeping the final choice with the human.
  • Support the development in Kazakhstan of a specialized legal framework for AI content and protection of creators’ copyrights.
  • Focus pilot projects on short formats and AI-friendly platforms for quick impact and demand validation.
  • Initiate the creation of local cultural datasets and registries of voices/instruments for proper model training.

Action Items

  • Prepare a package of proposals on AI regulation (copyright, licensing, fair use) for the relevant ministries of the Republic of Kazakhstan.
  • Launch a cross-industry working group on infrastructure (5G, data centers with GPUs, edge NPUs) to support real-time AI.
  • Organize training programs for creators and lawyers: generative pipelines, rights clearance, ethics and labeling.
  • Pilot interactive AI formats for TV/OTT (immersive shows, real-time viewer co-creation).
  • Roll out an initiative to collect and legally license local audio and video datasets (languages, timbres, instruments).
