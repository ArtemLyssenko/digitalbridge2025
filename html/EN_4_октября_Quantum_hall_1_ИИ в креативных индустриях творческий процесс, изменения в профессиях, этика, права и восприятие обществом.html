AI in creative industries: creative process, changes in professions, ethics, rights, and societal perception

Aika Alemi — Director, Born Nomad Incubator
Sholpan Makhmudova — Director of the sectoral department at the Center for the Development of Creative Industries Almaty Creative, curator of TEDxAlmaty
Yulia Kushnir — Head of TikTok in Central Asia
Anton Kim — Chief Marketing Officer, Forte Bank
Dmitry Sinitsa — Director of Artificial Intelligence, BI Innovations

Annotation

The panel discussed the rapid integration of AI into Kazakhstan’s creative industries, from ideation to production and effectiveness evaluation. Key barriers were highlighted: regulatory uncertainty, a shortage of local datasets and cultural-linguistic nuances, as well as risks of loss of context and ethical challenges. Practices for cost reduction and content scaling using AI tools and mixed creative stacks were presented. Consensus: prioritize education, local models, and ethics/brand safety standards while preserving the uniqueness of human creativity.

Goals

  • Assess the impact of AI on the creative industries globally and in Kazakhstan
  • Identify key obstacles: legal, ethical, linguistic, and educational
  • Discuss effective tools and cases of AI implementation in production and marketing
  • Formulate approaches to rights, authorship, and brand safety in the AI era
  • Outline joint steps with industry and the government (МинИи) to develop the local AI ecosystem

Key Questions

  • How is AI changing the creative process and production chains in Kazakhstan and the region?
  • What are the main barriers to adoption (legislation, rights, ethics, data, education)?
  • How can authenticity and cultural code be preserved when scaling creativity with AI?
  • Who owns the rights to AI content, and how to manage the risks of deepfakes and fraud?
  • Which practices genuinely increase efficiency (CTR, CPL) and reduce costs in performance marketing?

Key Points

  • Kazakhstan shows a high volume of video content creation; AI is already embedded at all stages of production (idea—script—video—evaluation).
  • Practical tools (TikTok Symphony, CapCut, AI avatars) accelerate production, enable content localization into dozens of languages, and forecast metrics.
  • A shortage of local datasets and the Western cultural bias of leading LLMs limit relevance for the Kazakh language and regional contexts.
  • Legal questions of authorship of AI content and brand safety remain unresolved; the risks of deepfakes and fraud are increasing.
  • Education and accelerated retraining (from early school to project managers) are critical for conscious and safe use of AI.
  • Implementing AI cuts production costs by 25–35% and increases campaign performance by ~30–40% with a mixed creative portfolio (UGC + pro + AI).
  • Concern: 'loss of context' and weakened transfer of expertise to junior staff with excessive reliance on assistants.
  • Need for transparency in budgeting and accounting: AI can become a mechanism for control and analytics of fund allocation.
  • A new market of roles: AI assistant managers, AI creators, curators of cultural-linguistic quality; at the same time, the uniqueness of human creativity is a key asset.

Conclusions

  • AI has become a systemic tool for the creative industries, but without local data, ethics, and education its effect will be limited and risky.
  • A 'human + AI' strategy, grounded in cultural code, transparent financing, and legal certainty, ensures growth in content quality and effectiveness.

Decisions Made

  • Focus on creating and enriching local datasets (Kazakh and regional languages) and supporting KAZ LLM.
  • Launch AI education programs for creative specialists and managers (tools, ethics, rights, brand safety).
  • Establish industry standards for checking cultural-linguistic quality (accent, stress, local norms) for AI content.
  • Initiate a working group with the Ministry of Artificial Intelligence on authorship, deepfakes, and the regulation of AI in advertising/media.
  • Recommend a mixed creative portfolio (UGC + professional production + AI) to optimize costs and improve performance.

Action Items

  • Compile a registry of AI tools for the creative industries (TikTok Symphony, CapCut, Notion AI, etc.) and run a series of practical workshops.
  • Launch pilots on localization and auto-dubbing with audits by native speakers and brand safety checklists.
  • Organize collection and labeling of local data (text, audio, visual) to further train models on culturally relevant examples.
  • Prepare guidelines on copyright and AI ethics, including procedures for responding to deepfakes.
  • Conduct A/B tests of AI creatives versus classic and UGC, tracking effects on CTR/CVR/CPL and costs.
  • Implement AI analytics in budgeting and project control processes (transparency of fund allocation).
  • Develop a mentorship program for young creatives to reduce the risk of 'loss of context' when working with AI assistants.
  • Communicate cultural code and art history in training creators, integrating local references into prompt engineering.
