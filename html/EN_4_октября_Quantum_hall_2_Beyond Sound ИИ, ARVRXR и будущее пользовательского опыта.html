Beyond Sound: AI, AR/VR/XR and the Future of User Experience

Almagul Menlibayeva — Contemporary artist
Yuldus Bakhtiozina — Director, artist
Kristina Khara — Multimedia artist, educator, curator
Hugo — Media artist, founder of a platform for AI media (international guest)
Aleksandr Mezin — Coordinator of the KBTU Game Lab mentorship program

Annotation

The panel discussed how AI accelerates development and changes UX in VR/AR/XR and games, while the experience remains constrained by platforms. The central theme was the boundaries of authorship and machine co-authorship: AI as a tool and as a collaborator. They examined cultural and ethical challenges—the need for local datasets, labeling of bots, and preserving collective experience amid personalization. Forecasts for the next 5–10 years were voiced: growth of interactive and empathic environments, institutional legitimation of digital art, and a priority on self-education.

Goals

  • Understand how AI transforms user experience in AR/VR/XR and gaming
  • Define the boundaries of authorship and the artist's control when working with generative tools
  • Identify cultural and ethical risks in AI- and virtual environments
  • Discuss the role of institutions (museums, galleries, universities) in legitimizing and implementing digital art
  • Formulate adaptation practices: local datasets, personalization without loss of collective experience, and educational approaches

Key Questions

  • How is AI changing the user experience in VR/AR/XR and the game industry?
  • Where is the boundary of authorship and the artist's control when working with AI?
  • How does AI support the development of personalized scenarios and learning environments in games?
  • What ethical and cultural challenges arise in AI-based virtual worlds?
  • What will the future of AI combined with AR/VR/XR look like over a 5–10 year horizon?

Key Points

  • AI accelerates iterations and prototyping and improves development quality, but UX is still limited by platform capabilities (platform bottleneck).
  • In art, AI turns passive viewing into an interactive experience (chatbots, quests, generative works), strengthening the link between the physical and the digital.
  • Authorship is a spectrum: from tool to collaborator; training models on your own data and maintaining control over the narrative and language helps preserve style.
  • Media complexity affects AI's role: in 'simple' media the sense of presence is valuable, in 'complex' ones (games) greater automation of code, dialogues, and assets is acceptable.
  • AI adoption faces a psychological barrier: creators often return to familiar tools so as not to lose a sense of control.
  • Cultural relevance of data is critical: Taiwan's example with 21 LLMs for 21 languages; for Kazakhstan it is important to account for the multilingual context.
  • We need to preserve collective experience amid personalization and explicitly label where it's a human and where it's a bot to avoid role confusion.
  • LLMs are not only a tool but also a new medium; specialized 'creative' models are still rare and remain underground.
  • Institutions and markets must legitimize digital art: integrating digital practices into museums/galleries and developing new forms of distribution (including games).

Conclusions

  • AI expands creative and UX capabilities but requires mindful control of authorship and narrative.
  • Local and culturally relevant datasets are key to high-quality and ethical content in a multilingual environment.
  • LLMs form a new medium while reinforcing existing ones; ideas and experience matter more than the toolset.
  • The legitimation of digital art depends on the active role of museums, galleries, and educational practices, as well as on mass self-education.

Decisions Made

  • Focus museum and gallery programs on integrating interactive and AI-assisted practices.
  • Promote the use of locally relevant datasets and language models for the Kazakhstani context.
  • Formulate transparency principles: explicit labeling of 'human/bot' interactions in artistic and educational projects.
  • Support self-education and knowledge-sharing practices (open tutorials, workshops) as the primary mechanism of adaptation.

Action Items

  • Develop prototypes of personalized museum chatbots and run pilot showcases with visitor feedback.
  • Collect and curate local datasets (Kazakh, Russian, etc.) for text and multimodal models relevant to the cultural context.
  • Prepare methodological materials for artists: a guide on authorship, quality control, and ethics when working with AI.
  • Organize a series of workshops on creating interactive XR experiences with AI (dialogues, multi-agent characters, asset generation).
  • Implement a transparency policy: label bots and automated content in exhibition and educational environments.
  • Create a practitioner community (lab/club) at KBTU and partner institutions for exchanging developments and expertise.
