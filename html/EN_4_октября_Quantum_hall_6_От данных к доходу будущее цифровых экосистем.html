From Data to Revenue: The Future of Digital Ecosystems

Tazhikenov Madi — Program Manager, Silkroad Innovation Hub
Den German — Expert in innovation policy and a filmmaker covering technology and the changing global economy
Arman Abdrasilov — Head of the Situation Center of the Security Council of the Republic of Kazakhstan
Zhanar Samaeva — Chair of the Management Board of JSC National Payment Corporation of Kazakhstan
Kanafin Baizhan — Private Company 'Documentolog Global Limited' - Chair of the Management Board

Annotation

The panel linked the global context of 'data as an asset' with Kazakhstan’s practice: from valuing data and the rights to them to specific AI use cases in the public sector and business. Legal gaps were discussed — the shift from infrastructure-focused to data-centric regulation, open banking, and cross-border interoperability in Central Asia. The private sector showcased platform and AI assistant strategies ('winner takes most'). The eOtinish platform demonstrated how the flow of citizen appeals turns into managerial decisions; the main challenge identified was the speed of change.

Goals

  • Define mechanisms for moving from data accumulation to monetization and GDP growth.
  • Form principles of data-centric regulation (valuation, rights, security, and data exchange).
  • Launch a move toward open banking and a competitive financial ecosystem.
  • Define the role of AI in accelerating the digitization of G2G processes and optimizing the state apparatus.
  • Align regional (Central Asia) interoperability of ecosystems and data exchange.

Key Questions

  • How can Kazakhstan turn data into revenue and find a focus of competitive advantage?
  • Where is the balance between maximizing data use and national security?
  • Who owns banking data, and how do you launch open banking without compromising security?
  • How do monetization strategies evolve from local solutions to regional/global competition?
  • How do you turn the flow of citizen appeals into sustainable public value and increased trust?

Key Points

  • Technology and AI have been democratized: new centers of competence are emerging outside the West (example: Uruguay, South Korea, Kazakhstan).
  • Data are the 'new oil' by analogy with refining: value arises from synthesizing secondary data and products.
  • Kazakhstan needs a pivot to a data law: the current regulatory framework is oriented toward infrastructure rather than data as an asset.
  • Data rights and IP are the main bottlenecks of global competition; access to data lakes is critical for industries.
  • Valuing personal data: a benchmark of 10% of a person's annual income; at the country level, it's closer to 10% of GDP.
  • G2G processes dominate and remain non-digitized; at the current approach, full digitization will take 'millennia' — AI and a paradigm shift are needed.
  • Platform economy 'winner takes most': the battle for user attention in a single window/assistant (the phone as the main arena).
  • Open banking requires client consents, standards, a regulatory club, and backbone infrastructure; barriers are regulation and readiness for competition.
  • eOtinish (2.2 million users, up to 5 million appeals per year) uses NLP/AI for sentiment and auto-replies; the key risk is the speed of scaling.

Conclusions

  • A shift to data centricity is necessary: legal status, valuation, and economic integration of data as an asset.
  • AI is a tool to accelerate G2G digitization and increase the productivity of the state apparatus and business.
  • Open banking and ecosystem interoperability in Central Asia are key to competition and scaling.

Decisions Made

  • Initiate the development of a concept and a draft data law (valuation, rights, exchange, security).
  • Expand AI pilots in the state apparatus: auto-summarization and answer generation in eOtinish, optimization of G2G processes.
  • Create an interagency 'regulatory club' on open banking with a roadmap of standards and consents.
  • Prepare a proposal on regional interoperability: a Central Asia working group on data exchange and interoperable data lakes.

Action Items

  • Conduct an inventory of state data assets and approve a methodology for their valuation.
  • Launch a national data lake with access management and lineage; connect priority information systems via a unified data catalog.
  • Deploy a regulatory sandbox for open banking (API standards, Consent Management, anti-fraud).
  • Scale AI assistants: answer generation, request summaries, routing, and SLA monitoring in eOtinish.
  • Form a cross-government G2G digitization program with KPIs for reducing processes and time.
  • Prepare a communications program for citizens about data rights and the benefits of sharing with security guarantees.
