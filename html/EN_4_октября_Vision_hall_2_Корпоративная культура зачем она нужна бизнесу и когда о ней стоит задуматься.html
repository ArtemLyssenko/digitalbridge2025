Corporate culture: why does a business need it and when should you start thinking about it?

Ksenia Toropova — CEO of HR BOX Kazakhstan
Beibit Baktygaliev — CEO of Atlanti.ai
Renata Nureeva — CEO & Founder of ProHunters (Recruitment & Executive Search agency)
Aldongarov Zhaslan Sembovich — Chief Executive Officer of Robolabs

Jerome Boye — Managing Director for the Western and Central Asia cluster at Alstom

Annotation

The panel discussed corporate culture as a key advantage in the competition for talent: from startups to international corporations. Emphasis — personal development roadmaps and leadership principles (Amazon experience), candidates’ demand for innovation and well-being, working with zoomers through compelling products and flexibility. Global cultural standards are combined with local specifics, especially in highly regulated industries where it is important to balance innovation and safety.

Goals

  • Understand which elements of corporate culture truly attract and retain strong professionals.
  • Compare approaches to culture in startups and large companies, including international ones.
  • Figure out when and how to start systematically building corporate culture.
  • Identify the expectations of zoomers and AI specialists toward employers.
  • Show how to integrate innovation (AI) without compromising safety and regulatory compliance.

Key Questions

  • What do top candidates pay attention to besides compensation when choosing an employer?
  • How to attract and retain zoomers and AI developers in an early-stage startup?
  • When should a company start investing in corporate culture, and how should it be scaled?
  • How does a global corporate culture align with local market specifics?
  • Does corporate culture influence the trust of investors and long-term partners?

Key Points

  • Development roadmaps and training to global standards are a strong retention factor (practices from Amazon/Meta are ported into a startup).
  • ProHunters research: high candidate interest in companies related to AI and innovation; focus on well-being and development is a key trend.
  • For zoomers, interesting challenges, real user impact of the product, flexible schedules, and a clear hiring funnel are critical.
  • Startups attract through the founder’s personality and values; large companies — through predictable systems, career mobility ladders, and stability.
  • A leader is a role model: without their daily involvement, values “die” on the periphery of a large organization.
  • Culture must be codified into principles and processes (transparent goals, talent management), otherwise change stalls.
  • Global corporations maintain a unified culture but execute it through local teams, keeping a balance between innovation and strict regulatory compliance.
  • Culture is an evolutionary process: it changes as the company grows and as market challenges evolve.
  • Practical flexibility in the office environment and daily life (location, amenities, schedule) is part of the real culture of everyday experience.
  • Culture affects the trust of customers and investors: long-term commitments and resilience are important for partnerships and regulated markets.

Conclusions

  • Corporate culture is a practical tool for competing for talent: development, impact on the product, and well-being matter more than perks.
  • In large companies, culture rests on role-model leadership and formalized principles; in startups — on the founder’s personality and the interest of the problems being solved.
  • Global players maintain unified values but scale innovation through local teams, preserving a priority on safety and compliance.

Decisions Made

  • Agreed to communicate and formalize leadership principles and development roadmaps for employees.
  • Decided to strengthen the focus on employee well-being and transparent communication about management style.
  • Agreed to prioritize interesting, market-relevant AI tasks that increase developers’ visible contribution.
  • Decided to combine innovation with safety and regulatory requirements when bringing solutions to market.

Action Items

  • Audit current practices: formalize leadership principles and career development roadmaps for key roles.
  • Launch/continue participation in the study «Рейтинг работодателей Казахстана», collect employee feedback.
  • Embed flexible schedules for students/juniors and automate the hiring funnel (test assignments, auto-parsing).
  • Develop a well-being program: communications, prevention, access to specialists, participation metrics.
  • Create a mechanism for selecting and piloting AI initiatives with assessments of safety, compliance, and business impact.
