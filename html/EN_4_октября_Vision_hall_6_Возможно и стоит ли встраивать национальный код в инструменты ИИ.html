National code in the AI era: localization of Kazakhstan’s models and data

Kurmangaliyeva Bikesh — Chair of the Management Board of AO «Центр развития цифровой экономики»
Uteulina Aizhan — Candidate of Philosophical Sciences, certified coach
Abdrakhmanova Madina — Expert in artificial intelligence, Deputy CEO for Products and External Relations, Senior Researcher at the Institute of Smart Systems and Artificial Intelligence, Nazarbayev University, Chief AI Officer, ForestHero
Sedykh Elena — Venture investor, founder of Договор 24

Annotation

The panel discussed how to embed the Kazakh national code into AI through the localization of data and models. Practices were showcased: the open foundational model KazLM and multimodal solutions trained on local content. The business focus is model specialization (SLM), on-device/local deployments, and data governance for ROI and privacy. The urgency of collecting oral, cultural, and visual heritage was emphasized to avoid the dilution of identity.

Goals

  • Preserve and reflect Kazakhstan’s national identity in AI systems
  • Build large-scale local datasets (text, speech, image) and ensure their sovereign storage
  • Develop localized (including multimodal) models and tools in the Kazakh language
  • Reduce dependence on foreign platforms through local infrastructure and SLM/on-device approaches
  • Synchronize efforts of government, academia, and business for applied deployments with tangible returns

Key Questions

  • How to methodically “encode” the national code (language, values, symbols) into AI architectures and datasets?
  • Where and how to rapidly collect high-quality local data, especially oral and visual heritage?
  • How to balance data openness with sovereignty, privacy, and the ethics of use?
  • Which models (LLM, SLM, multimodal) and infrastructures are optimal for business and the public sector?
  • How to scale successful pilots and achieve real ROI from localized AI?

Key Points

  • National identity rests on three pillars: shezhire (memory of generations and responsibility), language/culture, and values (harmony with nature and society).
  • Data are the new national capital; without their systematic collection and control, identity in AI will be blurred.
  • Collecting oral heritage (speech, aitys, rituals) is critical: there is a shortage of modern Kazakh textual data.
  • The NU institute created KazLM (2024) — the first localized foundational model for the Kazakh language, available on Hugging Face.
  • To adequately reflect culture, multimodal models (speech+text+image), rather than only text LLMs, are needed.
  • Kazakh Wikipedia is a basic point for the entire AI world: it acutely lacks articles, audio, and images.
  • Infrastructure: from supercomputers at universities to assembling affordable servers and on-device model runs.
  • AI business value arises in applied use cases: automation, personalization, operational innovations, and new business models.
  • Global deployment statistics: 78% of companies claim to use AI, but about 70% of projects fail; the main problem is data.
  • Trends: shift toward Small Language Models and local/on-device deployments to reduce costs, increase privacy, and improve localization.

Conclusions

  • Without a systematic local data foundation and models, national identity in AI is inevitably distorted.
  • Localized models (including multimodal and SLM) are technically and economically feasible in Kazakhstan right now.
  • A focus on applied use cases and data management is the key to ROI, security, and sustainable scaling.

Decisions Made

  • Focus on collecting and digitizing local data (including speech and visual content) as a strategic priority.
  • Continue developing and openly distributing localized models (KazLM and multimodal solutions).
  • Develop and support local computing infrastructure and on-device deployments to ensure data sovereignty.
  • Stimulate the enrichment of Kazakh Wikipedia and open corpora as the basis for training global and local models.
  • Strengthen R&D–business partnerships for specialized SLMs and applied pilots with measurable impact.

Action Items

  • Launch a national program to digitize oral heritage (an audio corpus of aitys, tales, rituals) and visual culture.
  • Create a unified registry and showcase of open datasets (with licenses and metadata) for training local models.
  • Deploy pilots of SLM/multimodal solutions in priority industries (finance, healthcare, education, public services).
  • Develop methodological guidelines on assembling affordable servers and on-device model deployment for organizations.
  • Organize a training program for data engineers and MLOps with the participation of universities and industry.
  • Establish an expert group on data ethics and sovereignty to assess datasets and models before deployment.
  • Run a campaign to develop Kazakh Wikipedia: grants/volunteer initiatives for texts, audio, and images.
