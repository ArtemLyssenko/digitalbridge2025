Keynote speech: Solana: internet capital markets

Lily Liu — President of the Solana Foundation

Annotation

Lily Liu presented Solana's vision as a global internet layer for capital markets, where tokens provide 24/7 access to liquidity for everyone. She highlighted the structural problems of traditional markets: privatization of gains and socialization of losses, rising barriers to entry, and the lengthening of companies' private phase. Blockchain is framed as a "universal basic opportunity": data becomes money, which lowers thresholds for investors and issuers around the world, including Kazakhstan.

Goals

  • Show why Solana's speed and low transaction cost are strategically important for financial markets.
  • Justify blockchain as a mechanism for lowering barriers for investors and issuers at a global level.
  • Position Solana as the internet layer for direct listing and trading of tokenized assets.
  • Build understanding of 24/7 capital market access through wallets and tokens.
  • Stimulate interest from countries and companies (including Kazakhstan) in issuing and listing assets on the Solana network.

Key Questions

  • How to lower barriers to entry to capital markets for retail investors around the world?
  • How does blockchain turn data into money and create a 'universal basic opportunity' instead of a universal basic income?
  • How to give issuers (including SMEs and companies from emerging markets) direct access to global liquidity?
  • Why do traditional markets increasingly concentrate returns in the private phase, and how can this be countered?
  • How can Solana become a standard infrastructure layer embedded in browsers and mobile devices?

Key Points

  • Solana processes more transactions than all other blockchains combined and was originally built as a global financial system.
  • Traditional capital markets have shifted: companies stay private longer, which pushes financial freedom further away for the majority and concentrates returns among the few.
  • The 2008 crisis and the subsequent 'bailout' demonstrated the privatization of gains and the socialization of losses amid rising public debt and service costs.
  • Concentration of returns in the S&P 500: a few of the largest technology companies make up a significant share of the index, and 'alternative' assets are no longer alternative.
  • On the buy side, blockchain simplifies access: a wallet is created in a minute, after which stablecoins, memecoins, and a wide range of tokenized assets are available.
  • On the sell side, traditional listing is expensive (on the order of hundreds of millions of dollars in fees) and concentrated in the US; competing with NYSE/NASDAQ is difficult.
  • Internet markets on blockchain operate 24/7 with a global distribution network: today around 200 million participants, with a potential of 5.5 billion internet users.
  • Solana seeks to become the 'internet layer' for capital markets, enabling direct asset listing and seamless operation from browsers and mobile apps.
  • For Kazakhstan and other emerging markets, this is a chance to access deep global liquidity beyond local exchanges (e.g., AIFC) and to tokenize real assets (including mining assets).

Conclusions

  • Blockchain technologies, and Solana in particular, can radically lower barriers in capital markets for investors and issuers.
  • The model of direct listing and asset tokenization opens up 24/7 global liquidity, which is especially important for developing economies.
  • Shifting from UBI to a 'universal basic opportunity' via open networks provides more sustainable economic inclusion than redistribution.

Decisions Made

  • Focus on developing Solana as the foundational internet layer for financial markets and direct listings.
  • Continue simplifying the wallet user experience and access to a variety of tokenized assets via browser and mobile devices.
  • Expand global distribution reach, aiming to grow the audience from hundreds of millions to billions of users.
  • Support use cases for real-world sectors (including mining companies in Kazakhstan) to access global liquidity through tokenization.

Action Items

  • Launch tokenization pilot projects for companies from Kazakhstan (e.g., the mining sector), followed by direct listing on the Solana network.
  • Organize educational sessions on creating wallets, working with stablecoins, and key management for new market participants.
  • Form partnerships with local exchanges and fintech ecosystems (including AIFC) for issuer onboarding and integration of on/off-ramp solutions.
  • Prepare methodological materials on the legal and technical aspects of token issuance for SMEs and corporate issuers.
  • Expand the tooling infrastructure (SDK, API) for simple direct listing and token management from the web and mobile apps.
  • Conduct analysis and engagement with regulators on KYC/AML compliance within open blockchain markets.
