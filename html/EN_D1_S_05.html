Digital Transformation of Kyrgyzstan: Tunduk, the Digital Code, AI, and Interagency Interaction

Minister of Digital Development of the Kyrgyz Republic (name not specified) — Government of the Kyrgyz Republic

Annotation

The Minister presented the practical results of Kyrgyzstan’s digital transformation: the Tunduk eGov mobile application with 19 legally significant digital documents; the launch of an end-to-end digital birth registration service with interagency integration and subsequent medical services; the adoption of the Digital Code, which consolidated regulation of the internet, AI, and satellite communications and strengthened the protection of children’s data; the development of the AI ecosystem (Kyrgyz language model, supercomputers, data centers powered by “green” energy) and a strict shift to paperless public services through the interagency interaction system.

Goals

  • Share the experience and practices of digital public services of the Kyrgyz Republic
  • Provide legal certainty for businesses and citizens through the Digital Code
  • Accelerate the transition to a digital-first model and the phase-out of paper documents
  • Develop infrastructure for AI (language model, supercomputers, data centers) based on “green” energy
  • Attract investors to the country’s digital infrastructure

Key Questions

  • How to scale interagency integrations so that services become truly end-to-end?
  • What power capacities and financing models are needed for the sustainable development of data centers and supercomputers?
  • How to ensure the protection of children’s personal data and consent mechanisms in digital services?
  • What measures will ensure discipline among government agencies during the transition to paperless service?
  • How to support the private sector in developing and deploying AI models in the Kyrgyz language?

Key Points

  • The Tunduk application contains 19 digital documents (from an ID card to an OSAGO policy), recognized by law as digital-first.
  • The digital birth registration service is integrated with multiple agencies and automates the routing of medical services (vaccination, etc.).
  • The Digital Code of the Kyrgyz Republic has been adopted, unifying about 5 laws and ~100 subordinate acts; it covers the internet, AI, satellite communications, and data protection.
  • For the first time, the concept of “child’s personal data” and mechanisms of anonymized consent for services for children have been established.
  • The document received a positive assessment from the UN, strengthening the confidence of investors and partners.
  • A Kyrgyz language model has been developed and is being enriched; the state supports private-sector AI initiatives.
  • Supercomputers are being deployed with access for government agencies and businesses; additional procurements are planned.
  • Kyrgyzstan relies on water resources and “green” energy to power data centers and HPC infrastructure.
  • A decree has been adopted: government agencies are not entitled to require paper documents; confirmation is through the application and interagency data exchange.
  • The interagency interaction system enables agencies to obtain the necessary data on their own without citizens’ participation.

Quotes

Conclusions

  • The legal and technological foundations for a digital state in Kyrgyzstan have been created and are receiving international recognition.
  • The transition to paperless services and the development of AI infrastructure require interagency discipline and investment, but are already delivering tangible benefits for citizens.

Decisions Made

  • The Digital Code of the Kyrgyz Republic has been adopted as a single regulatory act in the digital sphere.
  • An end-to-end digital birth registration service with interagency integration has been introduced.
  • A government decree has been adopted prohibiting the demand for paper documents from citizens.
  • Supercomputers have been launched, and their capacity is planned to be expanded for government entities and businesses.
  • A Kyrgyz language model has been developed and is being rolled out to support AI solutions.

Action Items

  • Procure additional supercomputers and ensure access to them for government agencies and private companies.
  • Expand the list of digital documents and functions in the Tunduk application.
  • Deepen the data corpus and quality of the Kyrgyz language model (including historical sources).
  • Attract investment in data centers powered by “green” energy and ensure reliable power supply.
  • Fine-tune compliance control for the “no paper” policy and train front-office staff.
  • Scale interagency integrations and develop proactive public services (for example, vaccination).
