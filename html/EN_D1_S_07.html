Panel session: Telecom as a platform for the digital future

Таир Исмаилов — Director of Strategic Management at GSMA
Дамир Сейсембеков — Chairman of the Telecommunications Committee of the Ministry of AI Industry and Digital Development of the Republic of Kazakhstan
Аскар Жамбакин — Chief Executive Officer of Kcell
Александр Бабичев — Chief Executive Officer of Tele2/Altel Kazakhstan
Алексей Шаравар — Chief Executive Officer of QazCode

Annotation

The panel discussed how 5G, AI, and satellite technologies are shaping Kazakhstan's digital future. The government is betting on sovereignty in AI: local compute capacity, Kazakh-language LLMs, and an AI law. Operators showcased practical AI use cases for the network, anti-fraud, and customer experience, as well as private networks for industry. A notable segment was the role of NGSO satellites (Starlink, OneWeb, Kuiper) in closing digital "white spots" and future D2D services.

Goals

  • Accelerate the deployment of 5G and optical infrastructure with priority for regions and rural settlements
  • Ensure digital sovereignty in AI: local data centers/GPUs, Kazakh-language models, and legal regulation
  • Reduce digital risks for the population through anti-fraud and a safer internet for children
  • Develop the B2B track: private LTE/5G networks, AI solutions, and export of digital products
  • Form balanced regulation and spectrum policy for NGSO and promising D2D services

Key Questions

  • How does 5G rollout change subscriber behavior, traffic, and service structure compared to LTE?
  • What does digital sovereignty in AI consist of, and which infrastructure elements are critical for the country?
  • How are operators applying AI in the network, customer service, and security, and where is the maximum impact?
  • What role will private mobile networks play in industrial automation and safety?
  • What is the optimal role of NGSO satellite constellations, and what regulatory steps are needed for their integration?

Key Points

  • Kazakhstan has improved its position in the Ookla ranking and leads the CIS in MBB quality/speed; growth is driven by 5G, fiber, and backhaul modernization
  • Kcell and Tele2/Altel have joint RAN and 5G spectrum development; at the same time, differences in transport, cache, VoLTE, and IT yield a different customer experience
  • Average consumption for Kcell subscribers is ~30 GB, 5G traffic grew 3x; up to 40% of subscribers already use 5G, peak speeds at events are ~200 Mbps
  • Tele2/Altel applies AI in SON and predictive maintenance (200+ base stations), actively develops VoLTE (35% of voice traffic)
  • Beeline, through «Каскад», focuses on AI: KazLLM, an AI tutor in «Жанымдай», local GPU data centers ("Hyper Cloud Solutions"), and the corporate platform AVENTA
  • The state is creating conditions for AI: a supercomputer cluster has been launched, an AI law is being developed, and a Council under the President on AI implementation has been established
  • Anti-fraud and user protection: Kcell — blocking 2+ million calls over 9 months and 'highlighting' suspicious calls; Tele2/Altel — commercial launch of «Детской SIM»
  • Private LTE/5G networks in industry (KazMinerals, ERG, etc.) increase productivity and safety: autonomous machinery, precise automated control systems, local cybersecurity perimeter
  • NGSO satellites are used for backhaul and spot access: OneWeb is covering 500+ villages with Wi‑Fi, Starlink is officially in the Republic of Kazakhstan, Project Kuiper entry is being prepared; D2D is being discussed (starting with SMS/112)
  • The spectrum and data center agenda is expanding: bands have been allocated for NGSO on a 'no-harm and no-protection' basis, and strengthening of the Committee's role with AI infrastructure is planned

Conclusions

  • 5G, fiber, and local data centers with GPUs are the foundation of digital sovereignty and a platform for mass AI adoption.
  • The greatest short-term AI impact for operators is in customer service, anti-fraud, and network optimization; strategically, B2B and the export of IT products are growing.
  • NGSO satellites will complement cellular networks for 'fine-tuning' coverage and critical services (including D2D/SOS), requiring thoughtful regulation and spectrum coordination.

Decisions Made

  • Official launch of Starlink in Kazakhstan: availability of equipment and services for users.
  • Launch of the OneWeb program to connect 504 rural settlements to public Wi‑Fi by the end of the year.
  • A roadmap was signed by the Ministry of AI Industry and Digital Development of the Republic of Kazakhstan with Amazon on the implementation of Project Kuiper; «Казахтелеком» — distributor.
  • The Communications Law enshrined the licensing regime for NGSO; bands have been allocated for NGSO on a 'no-harm and no-protection' basis.
  • Tele2/Altel signed a memorandum to use AONIT's GPU capacity to accelerate AI use cases.
  • Commercial launch of the product «Детская SIM-карта» (Tele2/Altel) with content filtering and parental controls.
  • Implementation at Kcell of an anti-fraud system and 'highlighting' of suspicious calls in the app with mass spam blocking.

Action Items

  • Finalize adoption of the Artificial Intelligence Law and by-laws on ethics, data protection, and liability.
  • Accelerate connecting 3,000 villages to fiber under the agreement with «Казахтелеком» and provide backhaul for base stations.
  • Expand 5G coverage and the share of VoLTE, scale SON/predictive maintenance to all regions.
  • Deploy local GPU clusters and stimulate demand for AI through grants/preferences for data centers and developers.
  • Scale anti-fraud algorithms and call risk tags; create an industry center for sharing fraud indicators.
  • Advance D2D pilots (starting with SMS/112 in mountainous areas) jointly with NGSO operators and cellular players.
  • Expand private LTE/5G networks in mining and metals, oil and gas, and energy; develop standard requirements for cybersecurity and local processing.
  • Run digital literacy campaigns and encourage upgrading devices to 5G-ready models.
  • Form a spectrum policy for mass IoT and dedicated bands for smart devices and industrial traffic.
