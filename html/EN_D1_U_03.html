Mobile Data in a Changing World: New Metrics for Effective Public Administration

Maksat Turlubaev — Head of the Bureau of National Statistics of the Agency for Strategic Planning and Reforms of the Republic of Kazakhstan
Trevor Monroe — Senior Program Manager, World Bank, Co-Lead of GDF-MPD Program
Gerttu Pilsas — CEO, Positium
Daniel Serzhanuly — Acting Chairman of the Management Board of JSC NC 'Kazakh Tourism'

Annotation

The session showed how mobile data and AI are moving from pilots to institutional use in public administration. Kazakhstan presented legal changes and a tourism pilot where mobile data radically broaden coverage compared with traditional statistics. The World Bank described the global GDF/MPD program and countries’ path from pilots to sustainable data pipelines. Positium demonstrated practical cases for transport and social infrastructure; Kazakh Tourism outlined operational benefits for planning and investment.

Goals

  • Institutionalize the use of mobile data in official statistics and policy
  • Establish sustainable legal, organizational, and technical foundations for access to anonymized positioning data
  • Scale use cases beyond tourism (migration, transport, social protection, emergencies)
  • Ensure a balance between privacy and utility through standards, anonymization, and work with regulators
  • Build national capabilities (workforce, infrastructure, methodology) and public-private partnerships

Key Points

  • Kazakhstan carried out a digital transformation of national statistics and established a legal basis: amendments to the Law on State Statistics (2023) and to the Law 'On Communications' (27 September of the current year) to enable access to anonymized positioning data for statistical purposes.
  • The tourism pilot (БНС + Positium with support from the World Bank) showed that mobile data capture 107.6 million domestic trips versus 7.8 million officially recorded stays — 14 times more.
  • Seasonality and peaks of tourist load are visible at the level of days and hours (July–August, December, March, May), enabling rapid responses to fluctuations and incidents (e.g., floods).
  • Е-Қонақ covers only visitors registered in СМР and does not capture short visits and within-country movements; mobile data account for self-organized tourists and indirect demand.
  • БНС plans, starting next year, to receive full data from all three mobile operators, develop extraction rules, and introduce МД-based indicators into official statistics.
  • GDF/MPD builds two-year programmatic support: coordination with regulators and operators, training, UN/ITU methodologies, transition from pilots to sustainable pipelines and multiple use cases.
  • Mobile data complement surveys: big data increase the value of sample surveys, and surveys calibrate and reduce biases in big data.
  • Practical benefits: planning transport and social services (Positium cases: route redesign in Tartu delivered +20% ridership with the same budget), road infrastructure management based on traffic.
  • For privacy, anonymization, aggregation, and engagement with data-protection regulators are mandatory; standards and agreements (MoU, SLA, licenses) are being established.

Conclusions

  • Mobile data radically increase completeness, timeliness, and granularity of indicators on tourism, migration, and population mobility.
  • Sustainable use of МД requires a legal framework, privacy standards, interagency governance, and technological pipelines.
  • Integration of big data and sample surveys yields more accurate and cost-efficient statistics than each source alone.

Decisions Made

  • Starting next year, БНС will begin receiving full anonymized positioning data from all three mobile operators.
  • Develop and approve rules for extracting and using mobile data for statistical purposes at the national level.
  • Implement the pilot results into official tourism statistics and expand their application to migration and transport indicators.
  • Continue partnership with the World Bank and Positium for methodological and technological support.
  • Prepare methodological frameworks that allow МНO to legally monetize aggregated analytics for business while keeping macro-level statistics free of charge.

Action Items

  • Finalize MoU and SLA with all МНO, including regulations for anonymization, aggregation, and data transmission frequency.
  • Develop and publish a national methodology on МД in tourism, migration, and mobility in line with UNWTO and ITU recommendations.
  • Deploy secure processing infrastructure (DPI, ETL pipelines, quality control, privacy audit) at БНС.
  • Provide training for specialists of БНС and sectoral agencies on tools and interpretation of МД (including GDF/MPD courses).
  • Create dashboards for real-time monitoring of tourist flows and seasonal load on regional infrastructure.
  • Launch pilots on migration and transport (OD matrices, traffic by road segments) with integration of administrative and sensor data.
  • Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) jointly with regulators and approve procedures for responding to risks.
