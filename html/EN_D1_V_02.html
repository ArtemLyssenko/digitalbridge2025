AI at Scale: How Corporate Innovations Shape the Future of AI Adoption

Annotation

The panel focused on scaling AI in corporations: from infrastructure and sovereign compute to talent and the culture of use. They discussed the need to move to AI‑first, the democratization of AI for every employee, and implementation examples (local LLMs, the copilot approach). They emphasized the role of the state: cloud policies, cross‑border data exchange, and responsible regulation. Specific cases of infrastructure and services in Kazakhstan were presented: data centers, a supercomputer, 10 Gbit/s, Wi‑Fi 7, platforms for no‑code bots.

Goals

  • Identify the key barriers to scaling AI in large organizations
  • Show working corporate use cases that deliver measurable impact
  • Form an approach to sovereign AI infrastructure and local models
  • Develop principles for democratizing AI for all roles in the company (AI‑first)
  • Outline priorities of public policy to accelerate AI adoption

Key Questions

  • What is the main barrier to scaling AI in corporations: models, hardware, or people and processes?
  • How to combine humans and AI in investing and decision‑making to gain profit and reduce risks?
  • What IT infrastructure (data centers, networks, chips) is needed for sustainable and sovereign AI development in the country?
  • How can telecom operators ensure a zero‑click experience and an AI‑first culture for customers and employees?
  • What regulatory steps (cloud‑first, cross‑border data) are necessary to accelerate AI adoption?

Key Points

  • The main barrier to scaling AI is people and organizational processes; infrastructure is important, but competitive advantage comes from democratizing AI for every employee.
  • Corporate value of AI is unlocked through everyday scenarios: freeing up to 30% of working time and GDP growth of 2–4% with accelerated GenAI adoption.
  • Sovereign infrastructure is critical: local data centers, energy‑efficient cooling, access to GPUs/specialized chips, and national providers to retain economic value.
  • Kazakhstani telecom initiatives: launch of 10 Gbit/s access, Wi‑Fi 7 at 4–10 Gbit/s for the customer, a shift to zero‑click services and AI‑first training for 20,000+ employees.
  • АО «Казахтелеком» launched the «Альфа Радио» supercomputer (~400 NVIDIA GPUs) and an Activation Hub to support SLM/robotics and smart‑home solutions together with startups.
  • Beeline/Goscode is developing a local LLM (KZLM): the «Цифровой тимлидер» covers >70% of code reviews in CI/CD; ~30% of software is written with AI code; the Aventa platform enables no‑code creation of bots and BI insights in natural language.
  • In investing, “full autopilot” (Level 5) is unattainable today; the best approach is a copilot: human+AI multiplies productivity while maintaining accountability and the role of education.
  • Google’s global technologies (ML spell‑check, AlphaFold, transformers, Gemini) illustrate the scale of AI; the company is investing multibillion funds in AI infrastructure and workforce training.
  • Regulatory priorities: cloud‑first policies and streamlining cross‑border data exchange, taking into account international standards and a responsible balance of benefits and risks.

Conclusions

  • Scaling AI requires shifting the focus from technology to people: training, processes, an AI‑first culture, and democratization of tools.
  • Sovereign AI infrastructure and local models (including support for the Kazakh language) are a strategic asset for Kazakhstan.
  • Regulation should be enabling: cloud‑first and thoughtful rules for cross‑border data exchange will accelerate adoption and innovation.
  • The human+AI combination currently delivers maximum value in business, especially in research, code, and investment processes.

Decisions Made

  • Confirmed the bet on democratizing AI in corporations (from top management to frontline employees) and implementing an AI‑first culture.
  • Continue deploying sovereign AI infrastructure in Kazakhstan: water cooling in the Freedom Cloud data center and the «Альфа Радио» supercomputer in Almaty.
  • Expand the use of the local LLM (KZLM) in internal processes and pilots with partners, including code generation and CI/CD automation.
  • Launch and scale the Activation Hub of «Казахтелеком» for the selection and support of SLM/AI initiatives together with startups.
  • Focus corporate AI use cases on zero‑click customer experience, back‑office automation, and natural‑language BI analytics.
  • Strengthen cooperation with global technology companies in training talent and transferring responsible AI practices
