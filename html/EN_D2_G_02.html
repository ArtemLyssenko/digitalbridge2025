Fireside chat: How to increase the valuation of a real-world business through IT

Vitaly Volianiuk — Media expert and co-founder of Digital Business
Askar Baitasov — Founder of the abr+ lifestyle ecosystem and the ABR restaurant holding

Annotation

ABR’s practices for boosting valuation through IT are examined: transferring offline emotion into a digital experience and AI-based personalization; the role of the ABR Cloud management module for scaling the network and real-time control; investments of $3–4 million (target — $5 million) and growth of the IT team; a strategic shift to an open lifestyle ecosystem and monetization (paid reservations, subscription).

Goals

  • Show how IT increases the valuation of a traditional business using ABR as an example.
  • Transfer the guest’s offline emotion into a personalized digital experience using AI and hyperlocal content.
  • Enable scaling through a unified management layer (ABR Cloud) and real-time control.
  • Launch new monetization mechanics in the app (paid reservations, subscription).
  • Prepare to open the lifestyle ecosystem and rebrand the app to onboard external partners.

Key Questions

  • Will AI change the user experience in the restaurant industry, where emotions and atmosphere matter?
  • What is the value and uniqueness of ABR Cloud for a network with many concepts and operating partners?
  • How is the impact of IT investments ($3–5 million) measured on revenue, LTV, average check, and company valuation?
  • What is the scaling strategy: geography (Astana, Bishkek, Tashkent) and bringing IT products to market?
  • What are the lessons and mistakes of digital transformation: where to start and what to avoid?

Key Points

  • Emotion remains the core of the restaurant experience; the task is to translate it into the digital plane (phone, app).
  • A content factory and hyperlocal personalization based on LLM/AI have been created; data are anonymized and used safely.
  • Two key products: the guest-facing ABR Plus app and the ABR Cloud management module (data collection and real-time control).
  • In-house modules: delivery, POS, waiter, procurement; only the ERP module remains external (rewriting is not a priority).
  • Metrics: user base ~600k, MAU >150k; 75–80% of payments go through the app (QR); the average check in the app is ~$29, outside — 20% lower; ~20% of the base uses delivery.
  • IT investments already $3–4 million (plan — $5 million); team ~40 people at Astana Hub, planning 2x growth per year.
  • The IT division ('ABR Tech') is planned to become the main business in 3–5 years by revenue, surpassing offline.
  • Monetization: preparing to launch paid reservations and a subscription with privileges in Q4.
  • Geo-scaling: new properties in Astana; in Bishkek — 2 locations in 2025; for Tashkent — active location search.
  • Shift to an open lifestyle ecosystem: app rebranding, connecting external partners (restaurants, tickets, museums, cultural listings).

Conclusions

  • A strong digital platform and AI personalization increase customer value and enable real-time management of a multi-concept network, which directly affects business valuation.
  • Focusing on core user cases (payments, reservations) ensures quick adoption and monetization; complex B2B solutions without market readiness are better postponed.
  • Shifting the balance toward IT creates new revenue sources and sustainable scaling through an open lifestyle ecosystem.

Decisions Made

  • Launch paid reservations and a subscription with privileges for regular guests in Q4.
  • Increase the IT team roughly twofold over the next year.
  • Rebrand ABR Plus and open the ecosystem to external partners next year.
  • Expand in Astana, open two properties in Bishkek, and continue negotiations on locations in Tashkent.
  • Focus on developing the in-house ecosystem and not bring the management module to the external market until ready; postpone the ERP rewriting question.

Action Items

  • Refine the content factory and the anonymized data pipeline for LLM personalization (hyperlocal leisure scenarios).
  • Release paid reservations: define pricing, SLAs, cancellations and compensations; integrate into ABR Cloud and the app front end.
  • Launch the subscription: develop the privileges model (early bookings, bonuses, special menu), pricing, and billing.
  • Hire key roles (backend, mobile, data science, product, QA) to scale the team to ~80 people.
  • Integrate ticketing services, museums, and city listings; launch a unified leisure storefront in the app.
  • Connect external partners from hospitality: prepare the API/SDK, moderation and onboarding rules.
  • Expand ABR Cloud dashboards: opening checklists, stop-lists, top SKUs, kitchen statuses, and delivery SLAs in real time.
  • Prepare a rebranding plan: new name, positioning, design system, and communications campaign.
  • Close deals on locations: sign properties in Astana, finalize 2 sites in Bishkek, choose sites in Tashkent.
  • Form a roadmap for ERP: evaluation of rewriting vs. integration, risks, and budget.
  • Track metrics: MAU, share of QR payments, share of in-app revenue, reservation conversion, ARPU/LTV.
