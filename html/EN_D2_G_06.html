Key Points

  • Unique market opportunity: AI‑generated video is already showing higher engagement, and a significant share of social networks’ ad revenue will be directly tied to AI.
  • Scale vision: capturing ~10% of the aggregate revenue of social networks could enable a $1 trillion market cap; the goal is an accelerated path to $1 billion in revenue by 2028.
  • Strategy against Big Tech: a bet on daily product improvements, rapid hypothesis testing, and faster understanding of the new industry.
  • Winner‑takes‑all effect: one dominant player will remain in the niche; it will be extremely difficult for the rest to scale.
  • Team and culture: main hub in Almaty (63 people) and a team in California (7 people); a high workload of 70–100 hours per week is the norm for growth leaders.
  • Interest from major players: acquisition offers come in regularly, but the company is "not for sale".
  • Impact on the advertising market: AI democratizes video production — SMBs will be able to test dozens/hundreds of creatives per day with a single manager.
  • Market and careers: many new niches will appear (e.g., affordable 3D animation for social networks); by 2028 every second video on social networks may be AI‑generated.
  • Hiring in Kazakhstan: not only R&D developers are needed, but also finance/legal/back office; stock option packages above the 90th percentile of the regional market.
  • Kazakhstan’s ecosystem: strong English proficiency and consumer case studies, but a deficit of enterprise‑sales culture for Western markets — this is the key growth area.
