Fireside chat: How local digital services can successfully compete with global Big Tech

Timur Shalekenov — CEO - Yandex Qazaqstan
Vitaly — session moderator, Digital Bridge

Annotation

Discussed the strategy of Яндекс Казахстан: scaling through open collaborations, the launch of a venture fund, and flexible partnership models (rev-share, minority deals, M&A). Showcased applied AI cases with measurable impact in products and urban infrastructure (ambulances, traffic lights, municipal machinery). Highlighted regulatory challenges of platform work and the need to simplify taxation. Emphasized the role of the local ecosystem and integrators for fast go-to-market.

Goals

  • Show how local digital services can compete with global Big Tech for user attention
  • Discuss collaboration models between Яндекс Казахстан and local companies (technologies, integrations, investments)
  • Demonstrate practical AI effects in products and urban services
  • Align position with regulators on platform work and infrastructure digitalization
  • Announce plans to launch a venture fund and further M&A

Key Questions

  • Does Яндекс Казахстан have room to grow in the local market, and in which verticals?
  • How ready is business in Kazakhstan for collaborations, and which win-win cases already work?
  • What is the ecosystem strategy: revenue sharing, minority investments, M&A, venture fund — how and when?
  • Where does AI already deliver measurable results, and where have expectations not yet been met?
  • How do Яндекс technologies improve urban infrastructure, and what are the scaling plans (traffic lights, ambulances, municipal machinery)?

Key Points

  • Яндекс Казахстан has over 10 million unique monthly users across 15 services; among the active urban population, coverage is close to 100%.
  • Growth strategy — open collaboration: access to AI and cloud technologies of Яндекс for local integrators; already about 60 partners (including R-Style, Yoko).
  • Proven cases: voice assistants and robots at Technodom (increase in answer rate to ~85% without an NPS drop) and Kolesa (driving completion of listing publication).
  • Flexible ecosystem model: rev-share, API integrations, minority checks via the venture fund being created, as well as targeted M&A (acquisition of «Бери Заряд»).
  • AI is the core of the product strategy: personalization of «Моя Волна», recommendations in the Яндекс Go feed, optimization of urban mobility, neural answers in search.
  • Internal processes: 5–25% of code is already generated by AI on prompts; a shift from many dashboards to conversational analytics based on AI’s direct access to data.
  • Social impact: online tracking of ambulances; Kostanay is the first city with digitized traffic lights and a countdown; scaling is being prepared (Karaganda, Aktobe, then Astana/Almaty).
  • Monitoring of municipal machinery (e.g., snow removal): GPS tracking of routes and speed to reduce fraud and ensure transparency of budget spending.
  • Platform work: 105 thousand drivers and couriers paid 7.3 billion tenge; in total ~300 thousand active contractors. Яндекс acts as a tax agent (4% simplified regime), but the new Tax Code of the Republic of Kazakhstan carries risks (ban on combining regimes, status of self-employed couriers).
  • Team and expertise growth: offices in Almaty (hub) and Astana, rapid growth of the cloud direction (from 0 to 50+ employees in 1.5 years).

Conclusions

  • Competing with Big Tech requires local synergy: combining Яндекс technologies and the expertise of Kazakhstani integrators for rapid delivery of solutions.
  • AI delivers measurable value in products (personalization, logistics, search), while the impact on back-office processes will emerge as tools are widely adopted.
  • Яндекс Казахстан diversifies development instruments (venture fund, minority checks, targeted M&A) and remains open to various forms of partnership.
  • Fine-tuning regulation of platform work is needed (combining regimes, status of couriers, simplification of electronic document workflow) for market sustainability and tax transparency.

Decisions Made

  • Launch the Яндекс Казахстан venture fund on the МФЦА platform and present it by the end of the year.
  • Maintain an open partnership model and expand the pool of local integrators for joint development of end solutions based on Яндекс Облака and AI.
  • Scale the traffic light digitization project: after Kostanay, launch Karaganda and Aktobe, then expand to major cities upon agreement with regulators.
  • Develop neural answers in search and personalization in key products (Яндекс Музыка, Яндекс Go, Яндекс Еда) to strengthen market share.
  • Continue acting as a tax agent on the platform and seek simplified reporting for drivers and couriers.

Action Items

  • Finalize regulatory approvals for the venture fund (МФЦА) and hold a separate presentation with selection criteria and check sizes.
  • Publish the updated partner program for Яндекс Облака/AI and onboard new integrators from priority industries (healthcare, retail, transport).
  • Together with akimats, scale the integration of traffic lights and launch pilots in Karaganda and Aktobe, followed by a roadmap for Astana and Almaty.
  • Deploy a municipal machinery monitoring system (GPS, speed/route control) in pilot districts and prepare a report on budget savings.
  • Initiate a working group with the Ministry of Finance/State Revenue Committee on the Tax Code: allowing the combination of regimes, legalization of self-employed couriers, recognition of the online receipt as a closing document.
  • Accelerate internal AI tool adoption: set target shares of AI-generated code by team, train employees in conversational analytics and routine automation.
  • Expand the use of recommendation models in Яндекс Еде and Go to increase LTV and reduce delivery times, and fix product KPIs.
  • Strengthen market communications: case showcases (Technodom, Kolesa, med assistant) and open SDKs/APIs to speed up implementations.
