Central Asia at the intersection of global flows: building a unified regional ecosystem for the development of AI startups

Magzhan Madiev — CEO, Astana Hub (Kazakhstan)
Rajabov Jakhongir — Director for Business Development, IT Park Uzbekistan
Dauren Rakhimzhanov — Managing Director, Directorate of Digital Initiatives, ЕАБР
Ragim Bayramli — CEO SABBAH.HUB
Chubak Temirov — Director of the High Technology Park of the Kyrgyz Republic
Bakyt — HIT Forum organizer / technopark representative (Central Asia region)

Annotation

The panel discussed turning Central Asia and the Caucasus into a single technology and AI hub: joint promotion of the region and exporting startups under a common brand, aligning regulation for a single market, developing regional venture (including a fund of funds) and scaling talent through shared educational and research programs with global partners, backed by shared infrastructure (HPC/AI Super Cloud) and overseas hubs.

Goals

  • Position Central Asia and the Caucasus as a single global center for AI and startups
  • Establish a common market and rules of the game for scaling startups across the region
  • Develop regional venture infrastructure (a fund of funds and joint VCs)
  • Create an end-to-end pipeline of AI talent and R&D through partnerships with leading universities
  • Unify infrastructure (HPC, AI Super Cloud, accelerators, overseas hubs) for shared access

Key Questions

  • How to promote the region’s competitive advantages in the global market under a single brand?
  • What steps are needed to create a unified AI-startup ecosystem and a single market?
  • How to optimally structure regional venture instruments (including a fund of funds)?
  • Which model of joint AI-talent training and research is most effective?
  • With which global research centers should we build sustainable partnerships and exchanges?

Key Points

  • The region has strong demographic and educational potential, political stability, and a favorable geography between the East and West
  • Astana Hub and IT Park Uzbekistan are already jointly promoting startups as "Central Asia", opening overseas branches and launching joint acceleration programs
  • ЕАБР, through the Digital Initiatives Fund, supports integration-oriented digital projects with grants, investments, and loans; an Astana Hub grant for an AI platform and AI Super Cloud has been signed
  • A single market requires harmonization of terms, definitions, and regulation so that scaling from one country would automatically open up the whole region
  • Regional venture funds and participation of institutionals as LPs in a fund of funds are needed to multiply capital
  • Coordination is paramount: a shared roadmap of events, promotional activities, and joint programs reduces duplication and amplifies impact
  • AI has leveled the playing field for regions: small teams are able to create globally competitive solutions; the example of a local unicorn confirms the potential
  • It is critically important to shift the focus of education toward AI engineering and R&D; launch TOT programs for instructors and scale online learning
  • The researcher diaspora and partnerships with MIT, Stanford, Cambridge, ETH Zurich, KAIST, KOICA, etc. can accelerate knowledge transfer and the launch of deep tech
  • Shared infrastructure (supercomputer, AI Super Cloud) and joint centers should serve the regions, not individual countries

Quotes

Conclusions

  • Unified branding and coordination of countries deliver a multiplier effect in promotion and access to capital and talent.
  • Harmonized regulation, joint funds, and shared infrastructure are key to scaling AI startups.
  • A shift to AI education and R&D, as well as targeted engagement of the diaspora and global partners, will accelerate the formation of deep tech competencies.
  • The region is ready to move from ideas to implementation thanks to already signed agreements and active programs.

Decisions Made

  • An ЕАБР agreement has been signed to provide grants to Astana Hub for a regional AI platform and AI Super Cloud
  • The parties confirm the continuation of joint promotion of startups under the "Central Asia" brand and the expansion of overseas branches
  • Mutual participation of Astana Hub and IT Park Uzbekistan representatives in venture fund investment committees has been maintained and expanded
  • An agreement has been reached to discuss, after the forum, the parameters of a joint regional AI VC fund
  • Agreed to promote a shared roadmap of events and integrate new regional technoparks into the collaboration

Action Items

  • Form a working group to harmonize terms and regulation for a single market for AI startups in the region
  • Compile a unified calendar (roadmap) of all key events, acceleration programs, and promotional activities, eliminating overlaps
  • Develop the structure of a regional venture fund (or fund of funds) with participation of ЕАБР and other LPs
  • Launch region-wide AI educational tracks: TOT for instructors, online courses, and internships
  • Open access for the region’s startups to shared compute resources (HPC/AI Super Cloud) and set out the terms
  • Initiate partnerships with MIT, Stanford, Cambridge, ETH Zurich, KAIST, and attract researchers from the diaspora
  • Expand the network of overseas hubs (USA, Europe, MENA, Southeast Asia, China) under the region’s single brand
  • Align and launch joint accelerators in AI and deep tech with unified selection criteria
