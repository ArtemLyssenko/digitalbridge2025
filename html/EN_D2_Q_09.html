Central Asia at the intersection of global flows: building a unified regional ecosystem for the development of AI startups

Rupert Adam — Commercial Director, GITEX, Dubai World Trade Centre

Annotation

GITEX announced a global expansion with a priority on Central Asia and a partnership with Kazakhstan and Astana Hub to attract technology giants and venture investors. Kazakhstan was chosen as the region’s leading economy and a point of integration for Central Asian countries, as well as an East–West bridge. The focus is on talent and universities, cybersecurity, data centers, cloud, and supercomputing for AI. A startup-oriented event is planned in Astana with national pavilions and participation from about 60 countries.

Goals

  • Bring global technology corporations and investors to Kazakhstan and the Central Asia region.
  • Launch an international startup-oriented event in Astana with participation from ~60 countries.
  • Build and scale an ecosystem in cybersecurity, data centers, cloud, and AI supercomputing.
  • Develop and retain talent through engagement of universities and youth.
  • Strengthen regional cooperation among Central Asian countries for joint digital transformation.

Key Questions

  • How to synchronize the efforts of the countries of Central Asia (Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Armenia) to achieve common digital goals?
  • Which infrastructure conditions (connectivity, data, data centers, cloud) are critical for scaling AI initiatives?
  • Which model for attracting and retaining talent involving universities is most effective for the region?
  • How to incentivize the entry of global corporations and venture funds into Kazakhstan and its neighboring markets?
  • How to balance data openness with rising cybersecurity risks?

Conclusions

  • Kazakhstan is ready to serve as a regional magnet for technology, capital, and talent for Central Asia.
  • GITEX’s startup-oriented model, with an emphasis on infrastructure and cybersecurity, is aimed at accelerating digital transformation.
  • Broad international cooperation (60+ countries) and East–West connectivity are critical success factors for the initiatives.

Decisions Made

  • Launch an international GITEX event in Astana next year with a focus on startups and investors.
  • Form a partnership with Astana Hub and relevant government bodies on tracks for talent, cybersecurity, and infrastructure.
  • Include national pavilions of the countries of the region and aim for participation by at least 60 countries.
  • Make it a priority to attract global venture funds and technology corporations (Microsoft, Google, AWS, G42, etc.).
