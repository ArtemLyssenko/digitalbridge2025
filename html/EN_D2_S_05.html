Yandex panel session: Business growth tools: how BigTech sees the application of AI

Sergey Sorokin — Director of Business Development for Yandex Cloud in Kazakhstan and Central Asia
Aleksandr Borishevich — Director of Business Development for ML&AI at Yandex Cloud in Kazakhstan and Central Asia
Aleksandra Shkipina-Sanches — CBDO CIS, Yandex Retail/Tech
Kairat Aitpaev — Chief Data Officer, Globerce Capital
Aleksandr Nagorny — CPO, Kolesa Group

Annotation

The panel discussed practical AI use cases in Kazakhstan: contact center automation, speech analytics, and computer vision in retail, as well as product solutions from Kolesa/Krysha. The speakers unfolded a build vs buy discussion: the talent shortage and the need for fast time-to-market push companies toward platforms and SaaS. The emphasis was on data as the fuel for AI, compliance with the law of the Republic of Kazakhstan, and ROI metrics. AI is viewed as a copilot and an invisible layer of UX, requiring cultural change within companies.

Goals

  • Analyze local trends and priority AI use cases for Kazakhstan and Central Asia
  • Define metrics for AI’s impact on P&L and customer experience and implement A/B testing practices
  • Form a data approach: collection of text and audio data, labeling, security, and compliance with the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan
  • Develop a position on build vs buy: where platforms/SaaS are appropriate and where in-house models are
  • Outline organizational changes and competencies (copilots, AI agents) for scaling AI

Key Questions

  • Which industry demands for AI dominate in Kazakhstan, and where is the real economic effect visible?
  • How applicable are AI use cases for SMB compared to enterprise, and how does the implementation economics change?
  • Why do companies choose platform and off-the-shelf solutions instead of building from scratch on open source?
  • How to prepare and annotate data (especially text/audio) in light of the Republic of Kazakhstan’s law on AI and personal data?
  • How to measure the success of AI initiatives, and what to do with the time freed up for employees?

Key Points

  • Contact centers: chat/voice assistants trained on corporate data handle up to 80% of typical requests and save up to 30% of costs; employees focus on complex cases
  • Sales speech analytics makes it possible to analyze thousands of dialogues, highlight patterns, and increase team efficiency by 10% or more
  • In retail, computer vision (AI Inventory) increases shelf availability and revenue; the approach also applies to agriculture, oil and gas, and manufacturing
  • The talent shortage and demand for fast time-to-market push toward platforms and SaaS with proven algorithms
  • Data is a key asset: early collection of text/audio data and proper preparation of transcripts are required; otherwise, training datasets are unusable
  • The law of the Republic of Kazakhstan on AI requires protection of personal data and limits emotional manipulation — this affects the design of voice assistants
  • The metric model is two-layered: business metrics (conversion, CSI/CES, retention, FTE savings) plus AI/LLM quality metrics; pre-pilots and A/B tests are mandatory
  • Invisible AI in the product improves UX: price estimation, moderation, and recommendations work in the background, shortening time-to-value (for example, posting listings in 10–15 seconds)
  • AI infrastructure is expensive (GPU, MLOps), so platformization and cloud AI agents are relevant; in parallel, a mindset shift and employee training are needed

Conclusions

  • The economic effect of AI is achieved in applied, narrow use cases with measurable metrics, not in abstract LLM deployments for the sake of hype
  • A platform approach lowers the barrier to entry and risks, but data quality, security, and compliance with the law of the Republic of Kazakhstan are critical
  • Cultural change and developing employee skills in using copilots and AI agents accelerate scaling and ROI growth

Decisions Made

  • Launch pilots on cloud AI platforms with AI agents for specific narrow tasks instead of developing proprietary stacks from scratch
  • Implement a two-layer metric system (business + AI quality) and mandatory A/B tests to assess impact
  • Prioritize the collection and labeling of text and audio data from customer interactions for future model training
  • Design voice assistants in accordance with the requirements of the law of the Republic of Kazakhstan: protection of personal data and a ban on emotional influence

Action Items

  • Conduct a data audit: inventory sources, identify gaps, plan labeling of texts and calls; deploy a secure repository
  • Select 2–3 cases with fast P&L impact (L1 contact center, speech analytics, CV inventorying) and launch 6-8-week pilots on the chosen platform
  • Set up success metrics: conversion, CSI/CES, FTE savings; define LLM metrics and A/B testing processes
  • Organize team training: prompt engineering, personal data security, AI product analytics, and implementation best practices
  • Formalize compliance: DPIA, personal data retention policies, restrictions on assistants’ emotional influence
  • Deploy internal copilots for code, meetings, and documents (Fireflies, Нейроэксперт, GitHub Copilot, GPT) and track productivity gains
