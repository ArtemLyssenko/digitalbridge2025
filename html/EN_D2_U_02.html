Creating the Future: Seed Financing for AI Innovators

Pavel — panel moderator, Digital Bridge
Adil — managing partner of the venture fund Partners (Kazakhstan); early business angel

Tigran Bekmullin — Head of External Innovation, Managing Partner of Inmerge Ventures at Pasha Holding

Annotation

The panel focused on seed strategies and fund discipline, confirming the high potential of early stages amid heightened risks. They discussed how AI radically changes decision-making and why infrastructure and high-quality data are key to scaling. A separate segment was devoted to the role of the state: tax predictability, sandboxes, incentives, and AI self-regulation initiatives. They concluded with an emphasis on regional cooperation in Central Asia to stay “top of mind” for global investors.

Goals

  • Formulate effective seed investment strategies with a focus on B2B SaaS and retention metrics
  • Understand the impact of AI on corporate decision-making and the integration of startup solutions
  • Define the role of the state: regulatory sandboxes, tax incentives, infrastructure, and AI self-regulation
  • Strengthen regional coordination in Central Asia to attract global capital
  • Identify infrastructure priorities (DCs, GPUs, data) for scaling AI

Key Questions

  • How have deal terms and corporate structuring changed with the emergence of AIFC compared to “pre-AIFC”?
  • How is AI already changing managerial decisions in the public sector and corporations, and which cases are the most illustrative?
  • Why are open innovation and CVC more advantageous for corporations and banks than in-house development?
  • Which regulatory steps (sandboxes, tax stability) are critical for the growth of the ecosystem in the region?
  • What does it take for Central Asia to become a priority destination for global venture investors?

Key Points

  • Early stages deliver the greatest value growth but with high “mortality”; fund discipline and focus matter more than chasing hype.
  • AIFC has radically simplified the legal infrastructure of deals: standardized documents, regulator education, first cases.
  • AI radically changes the logic of decisions: from retro-analytics to patterns and predictive insights (smart city, seismic exploration).
  • Infrastructure (DCs, GPUs, supercomputers) and high-quality data are necessary conditions for the growth of AI solutions.
  • For corporate banks, it is easier to integrate external ready-made startup solutions than to build in-house; CVC accelerates transformation.
  • Regulation in the region is uneven: Uzbekistan lacks sandboxes; Tajikistan is advancing AI self-regulation and preferential regimes.
  • Tax predictability is critical: abrupt changes discourage outside investors from entering, despite forums and activity.
  • A global-first mindset from the start (example: Zipple.ai) enables companies from small economies to expand quickly into MENA and other markets.
  • Regional cooperation in CA (joint deals, unified forums, shared agenda) strengthens the region’s brand and capital flow.
  • Funds with LPs must follow the mandate and metrics (vertical, data uniqueness, architecture), while angels are more flexible and riskier.

Quotes

Conclusions

  • Seed investing is effective with strict focus (B2B SaaS, metrics, architecture) and funds’ restraint toward hype.
  • AI requires systemic investment in infrastructure and data; without them, there will be no scaling.
  • Central Asia needs to speak with one voice, ensuring stable policy and joint initiatives to get onto global investors’ radar.

Decisions Made

  • Continue fund focus on B2B SaaS and vertical AI solutions with unique datasets and verifiable architecture.
  • Launch and develop the Presight (G42) acceleration program, aimed at generating real traction through pilots with Big Tech.
  • Move the Central Region Venture Forum next year to Tashkent to strengthen the regional agenda.
  • Implement a PPP project for AI road safety analytics in Astana through to production deployment.
  • Create a specialized free zone for AI in Tajikistan with a 15-year incentives contract for residents.

Action Items

  • Form a CA working group to coordinate joint forums, cross-deals, and promote a unified agenda for global investors.
  • Initiate regulatory sandboxes in Uzbekistan and update tax predictability in Kazakhstan with industry participation.
  • Prepare and conduct an “awareness roadshow” in the US/Europe (Western LP/GP), promoting cases of Zipple.ai, Hixville, and corporate pilots.
  • Launch a regional plan to ramp up AI infrastructure (DCs, GPU pools, access to supercomputers) and data quality standards.
  • Expand the corporate pilots program: a bank in Uzbekistan as a testbed for fintech/regtech/risk-AI solutions.
  • Operate as a fund of funds in pilot mode: co-investment agreements with White Hill, Domino, etc., to enter international markets.
