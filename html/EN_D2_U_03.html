Series A as a maturity milestone: the role of founders, capital, and the ecosystem

Elzhan Kushekbayev — Venture investor and partner at MA7 Ventures
Murat Abdrakhmanov — Founder of MA7 Ventures, the largest venture investor in the region
Askar Bilisbekov — CEO of Qazaqstan Venture Group
Zhanadil Taldybayev — CEO of Paradigm Shift
Aleks Mashrabov — Co-founder and CEO of Higgsfield AI

Annotation

The panel discussed how startups can reach Series A: metrics, going global, and the role of focus. The need for an AI-first approach and readiness for pivots as a growth norm was highlighted. Higgsfield AI and Kodi Play unpacked practices of hyper-iterations, a hardworking culture, and global distribution. The Alem fund of funds announced a strategy to catalyze the ecosystem through investments in regional and global funds and hybrid corporate ventures.

Goals

  • Clarify metrics and readiness for Series A, including new market revenue thresholds
  • Show working strategies for entering global markets with R&D in Kazakhstan
  • Substantiate AI-first as the standard for products and business models
  • Discuss the role of a fund of funds in scaling the number of GPs and launching hybrid corporate venture funds
  • Highlight the value of rapid pivots and investor support at early stages

Key Questions

  • What metrics and thresholds are actually needed today to raise a Series A?
  • Which scaling route is more effective: to 'test' locally or go global right away?
  • What does AI-first mean in practice across different verticals, and how to avoid 'wrappers' without deep technology?
  • How will a fund of funds support first-time GPs, corporate funds, and the diaspora?
  • How do investors view pivots and what should a startup do if it's stuck at the seed stage?

Key Points

  • Series A is the point of proven product–market fit and the start of scaling; on average: valuation ~$50M, check ~$10M, revenue ~$10M.
  • The threshold to start a Series A dialogue has risen: in 2024 the benchmark is from ~$3M in annual revenue.
  • Only about 10% of those who raised seed reach Series A; virtually all successful cases from the region sell to global markets while keeping R&D in Kazakhstan.
  • AI-first is becoming a requirement: without adopting AI, businesses lose competitiveness and margin.
  • Higgsfield AI illustrates a hyper-iteration strategy (releases 5 times a week), focus, and distribution as a combined moat; the video-AI market is a new, fast-growing segment.
  • Kodi Play scaled to 13 countries thanks to partnerships with AI labs, social-network distribution, and a hardworking culture; in EdTech, demand is shifting from digital skills to AI as a subject.
  • Pivots are the norm: even large companies change vectors; lingering at one stage without multiples of growth is a signal to change market/model.
  • Copycat models in the local market are possible but hard to scale; the key is to plan the exit in advance and work with strategics.
  • Alem Ventures Fund (fund of funds) invests in local/regional/global funds, supports first-time GPs, and launches hybrid corporate venture funds with independent management.
  • The quality of the region’s human capital is a strong side; the diaspora and international LP/GP partnerships are important drivers of the ecosystem.

Conclusions

  • The Series A market has tightened: you need mature metrics, clear focus, and proven global distribution.
  • AI-first and readiness for rapid pivots are key differentiators of surviving companies.
  • A fund of funds and hybrid corporate venture funds can multiply the number of GPs and quality startups in the region.
  • Betting on human capital and the diaspora strengthens international integration and capital flows.

Decisions Made

  • Alem Ventures Fund focuses on investing in regional and global funds with an emphasis on first-time GPs.
  • Launch and scale a program of hybrid corporate venture funds managed by independent GPs.
  • Organize LP/GP educational initiatives jointly with top-tier funds to raise market expertise.
  • Support diaspora funds and initiatives investing in companies founded by people from the region.
  • The ecosystem is oriented toward a go-global strategy for startups from the very start.

Action Items

  • Founders: validate the path to Series A metrics (ARR ≥ $3M, growth, unit economics) and plan the round in advance.
  • Build an AI-first product: from integrating AI into core processes to developing your own models where it creates a moat.
  • Set a high iteration pace (weekly releases), retention and virality metrics; set horizons for pivots.
  • Go global from day one: target large markets and appropriate jurisdictions, build international distribution.
  • GPs and corporations: prepare independent governance structures for hybrid corporate funds and investment committees.
  • Alem/KVG: roll out LP/GP education and follow-up meetings after the LP summit; expand the AIFC license for the planned activities.
  • Network with the diaspora and top funds: form a pipeline of co-investments and talent scouting from the region.
