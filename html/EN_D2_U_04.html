AI as a Bridge: Cooperation between Startups and Corporations in Central Asia

Anuar Lenzat — CEO of RS Bootcamp
Alibek Sultangazin — Investment Director of Bglobal Ventures
Mukhammad Makhmudov — Director of Plug and Play Uzbekistan
Gulmira Akinbanshina — Executive Director at Bereke bank
Alim Khamitov — CEO of Most Ventures

Annotation

The panel discussed why in Kazakhstan and the region corporations remain the weak link in the startup ecosystem and how AI can become a catalyst for cooperation. The speakers highlighted systemic barriers: lack of strategic embedding of innovation, blurred accountability, and cultural resistance within corporations. Practices were presented: the venture client model at PASHA Holding, internal development and API partnerships at Береке Банк, as well as a B Global Ventures study on corporate innovation. The consensus: acceleration is possible through strategy, fast POC validation, standards and incentives, and the creation of exit channels (M&A/CVC).

Goals

  • Identify the reasons why corporations have not become key partners for startups in Kazakhstan and the region
  • Understand whether AI can become a catalyst and a “bridge” between startups and corporations
  • Shape workable engagement models: venture client, pilots, CVC, M&A
  • Define the role of the state and regulatory incentives to accelerate corporate innovation
  • Share case studies and practices for scaling pilots into production

Key Questions

  • Why do corporate innovation efforts develop slowly, and where is the breakdown — in corporations, startups, or regulation?
  • What changes in corporate strategy and structure are needed for AI and innovation to become a systemic part of the business?
  • Can AI become the connecting link and accelerator of pilots and deployments?
  • How to motivate industries with low competition and a high state share to innovate?
  • How to create more exit channels (M&A, CVC) and new “buyers” of startups beyond a handful of players?

Key Points

  • B Global Ventures presented the first industry study on corporate innovation in the region and stated the goal: awareness-raising and standardization of practices.
  • Corporate innovation must be part of the company’s 1–2 year strategy with regular reviews; AI is a cross-cutting theme, not a patch for current problems.
  • A clear responsibility matrix is needed: who inside the corporation owns which innovation process (HR, document workflow, ERP, analytics, etc.).
  • Cultural resistance (especially among middle management and the IT function) is one of the main stoppers; value for the rank-and-file employee is critical for adoption.
  • The exit market is narrow: many corporations want to be enterprise clients but not buyers; about 81% prefer the B2B model instead of M&A.
  • Береке Банк bets on internal development, SAFe, API partnerships, and acceleration; pilots quickly convert into scalable services (a case with MasterCard).
  • PASHA Holding combined startup acquisitions in the past but now focuses on the venture client model and subsidizing pilots in subsidiaries; M&A is not planned in the near term.
  • In highly competitive industries (banking, telecom) innovation moves faster; in sectors with a high state share, incentives and standards are needed (e.g., API standards, incentives for CVC).
  • The economic structure dominated by B2G constrains the development of B2B and demand for innovation; a shift toward the market and a stronger role for SMEs is needed.
  • Fast POCs (weeks, not months) and pinpoint scouting of solutions for specific pains are the key to successful cooperation (the Plug and Play approach).

Conclusions

  • Without strategic integration of innovation and clear internal accountability, pilots do not scale and do not lead to exits.
  • AI can become a practical “bridge” if used in specific processes (data, document workflow, HR, analytics) and if solutions are validated with fast POCs.
  • The role of the state is to create rules and incentives (standards, benefits for CVC), especially in low-competition industries.
  • The market needs new corporate “buyers” of startups and the venture client approach as an intermediate step toward M&A.

Decisions Made

  • B Global Ventures publishes an industry report on corporate innovation and collects feedback for subsequent iterations.
  • Береке Банк confirms its course toward internal development, SAFe, and API partnerships, scaling pilots through its own acceleration.
  • PASHA Holding maintains a focus on the venture client approach and subsidizing pilots in portfolio companies; no new M&A is planned in the near term.
  • Plug and Play will explore launching an industry program in Kazakhstan if there is corporate demand.
  • The panel organizers will pass the collected theses and recommendations to key corporations and relevant government bodies.

Action Items

  • For corporations: review 12–24 month strategies to include AI initiatives and KPIs for scaling pilots.
  • Appoint owners of innovation processes (HR/finance/operations/IT/analytics) and allocate budgets for venture client pilots.
  • Implement fast POC processes (weeks) and unified legal templates for pilots with startups.
  • For the state: develop industry-specific API standards and tax/regulatory incentives for CVC and pilots with domestic startups.
  • Launch training programs for corporate management (working with startups, ROI assessment, risk management, AI case studies).
  • For the ecosystem: expand the list of corporate buyers via industry clubs, case sharing, and public showcases of needs.
  • For funds and hubs: organize targeted scouting based on industry requests (agro, manufacturing, logistics) and benchmarking of exportable solutions.
  • Track metrics: share of pilots, conversion to contract/scale-up, POC time, number of corporate exits.
