Artificial Intelligence in Travel: efficiency, experience, and balance with empathy

Dudarov — Digital Bridge forum speaker; AI expert in the travel industry

Annotation

The speaker showed how AI is already changing travel: from biometric control and personalization to planners and assistants for hoteliers. He explained the exponential growth of adoption thanks to ready infrastructure and the user shift toward conversational interfaces. He emphasized that real implementation depends not only on technology but also on regulations and agreements. The main focus is a hybrid model: AI as the engine of efficiency, and humans as the source of empathy and meaning.

Goals

  • Show the role of AI as an engine of efficiency in the travel industry for customers and businesses
  • Explain the reasons for the exponential adoption of AI and the shift in consumer habits
  • Identify on-the-ground implementation barriers (regulation, agreements between countries)
  • Demonstrate practical cases of personalization and automation
  • Formulate the principle of balancing AI and human empathy in service

Key Points

  • Travel is part of the experience economy, where people buy not things but changes to their own identity
  • Technology has always accompanied travel: from the compass and railways to the internet and AI
  • AI adoption is growing exponentially thanks to already existing “roads” — mobile networks, the cloud, and data centers
  • e-gates case in Amsterdam: the technology exists, but without inter-country agreements and regulatory recognition it does not work (Kazakhstan’s passport is not supported)
  • AI in travel is an engine of efficiency: speeding up planning, personalizing recommendations, reducing costs
  • Practices: smart filters by free text, AI Trip Planner with native booking, the “Help me reply” assistant for hoteliers
  • Personalization has become the norm for customer expectations and increases loyalty and conversion
  • The trend of conversational interfaces and agentic commerce is growing: transactions directly inside ChatGPT
  • The future is agents interacting with each other (bot-client ↔ bot-support), while a hybrid approach with human empathy is needed

Conclusions

  • AI will penetrate more deeply into all stages of travel, but the key value of experiences and empathy remains with humans
  • The success of implementation is determined not only by the maturity of technologies but also by the readiness of the regulatory and partner environment
  • Conversational interfaces and agent platforms are becoming the new storefront for search and purchases in travel

Decisions Made

  • A course has been adopted toward a hybrid model of customer service (AI + human) as the basic quality standard
  • Expand pilots of personalization and AI assistants for travelers and hoteliers
  • Initiate dialogue with regulators and airports on recognition of biometrics and support for Kazakhstan passports in e-gates
  • Prepare for integrations with agentic protocols and transactions inside conversational platforms (for example, ChatGPT)

Action Items

  • Create a working group with government agencies and airports to develop agreements on biometric control and e-gates
  • Scale smart filters and AI Trip Planner to additional markets and languages; run A/B tests on impact to conversion
  • Launch training for hoteliers on using AI responses to guests with emphasis on tone, safety, and empathy
  • Conduct pilots of voice and “calling” agents in support, including bot-to-bot interaction scenarios
  • Implement monitoring of NPS/CSAT metrics vs. degree of automation and rules for manual escalation by triggers
