Annotation

The AI Manifesto was presented with an emphasis on human-centricity, openness, and AI safety. The vision of «Generative Nation» was introduced: a generative state, economy, society, and culture. The Prime Minister outlined measures: a new line ministry, the launch of «Alem AI», expansion of AI education, and the goal of $1 billion in IT service exports. The event concluded with the Digital Bridge Awards ceremony, recognizing the ecosystem’s best projects.

Goals

  • Make Kazakhstan a leader in the development and adoption of AI within the next three years.
  • Integrate AI into all key public services (Smart Data Ükmet, Kaz LLM, AI assistants).
  • Strengthen AI infrastructure and the talent base through universities, a national platform, and a Research AI University.
  • Accelerate the growth of IT service exports to $1 billion by the end of the current year.
  • Expand international cooperation and attract technology unicorns and investors.

Key Questions

  • How can transparency, explainability, and fairness of algorithms be ensured during large-scale AI deployment?
  • How to balance regulatory flexibility with the protection of human rights and data security?
  • How to deliver AI infrastructure and compute resources faster to developers, students, and startups?
  • Which mechanisms convert AI innovations into sustainable export growth and global competitiveness?
  • How to build interagency coordination to implement Smart Data Ükmet and Kaz LLM across all public services?

Key Points

  • The AI Manifesto enshrines values: human-centricity, openness, safety, and collaboration.
  • «Generative Nation» describes four dimensions: a state without barriers, an innovation economy, an inclusive society, and a culture of AI co-authorship.
  • Smart Data Ükmet is presented as the 'national brain' for analytics and managerial decisions.
  • Kaz LLM is the foundation of the digital dialogue between the state and citizens; AI assistants reduce the workload on civil servants.
  • The Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development has been created for the next technological leap.
  • AI programs have been launched at 20 universities; around 500,000 people are already learning using AI services.
  • The launch of «Alem AI» is a key element of the unified digital cluster for research and acceleration.
  • Astana Hub is strengthening venture support; Playrix, Mytona, and Netcracker have entered the country, confirming investor confidence.
  • Kazakhstan is 24th in e-government and 10th in online services; the goal is $1 billion in IT service exports by year-end.
  • Over 30 memorandums have been signed; Digital Bridge Awards were presented for export, AI solutions, EdTech, GovTech, Tech Media, and social impact.

Quotes

Conclusions

  • The course toward «Generative Nation» has been institutionalized: the human is at the center, and AI is an open and safe driver of development.
  • An infrastructure and education base (the ministry, «Alem AI», universities) has been formed for large-scale AI deployment.
  • The ecosystem demonstrates maturity through international partnerships, venture financing, and award-winning projects.

Decisions Made

  • Present and adopt the AI Manifesto as a framework document of values and principles for AI development.
  • Integrate AI into all key public services, leveraging Smart Data Ükmet, Kaz LLM, and AI assistants.
  • Strengthen the legal framework and support measures for specialists through the new line ministry.
  • Launch and scale «Alem AI» as the core of a unified digital cluster for research and acceleration.
  • Continue to develop Astana Hub and venture mechanisms to bring startups to the global market.
  • Implement the strategy to achieve $1 billion in IT service exports by year-end.
  • Concentrate interagency work in the digital headquarters under the government to promptly remove barriers.

Action Items

  • Prepare a roadmap for AI implementation in public services with KPIs for Smart Data Ükmet, Kaz LLM, and AI assistants.
  • Draft bills on algorithm transparency and explainability and on adaptive AI regulation.
  • Expand access to compute resources and data infrastructure for universities, startups, and developers.
  • Launch a national AI education platform and develop a program for the Research AI University.
  • Organize industry pilots (fintech, healthcare, education) based on «Alem AI».
  • Strengthen international partnerships and export programs to reach the $1 billion IT services goal.
  • Set up ongoing monitoring and reporting by the digital headquarters on removing regulatory and infrastructure barriers.
