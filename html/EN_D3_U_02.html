AI in game development: trends, tools, and strategies for Central Asian studios

Annotation

The panel brought together Central Asian gamedev leaders to discuss the impact of AI on game production. Speakers highlighted accelerated development and increased efficiency of small teams, a shift in market trends toward indie and service models, as well as the strengthening of metaverses and UGC/VR. Real cases of AI implementation were examined (level generation, matchmaking, balance, support), organizational structures for AI pilots, and legal protection of IP.

Goals

  • Capture key global and regional gamedev trends with a focus on AI
  • Show practical cases and metrics of AI adoption in production, support, and design
  • Discuss resilient business models: service games, UGC/metaverses, mobile segment
  • Define approaches to organizational AI adoption (skunkworks units, processes, tools)
  • Identify legal and IP risks (patenting, licensing) and ways to mitigate them

Key Questions

  • Which AI trends and tools are already changing development and the economics of gamedev studios?
  • Will AI replace developers, and what new roles will emerge (prompt engineering, etc.)?
  • How to safely integrate AI in large companies with legacy processes?
  • Which monetization and retention models are more resilient: AAA F2P vs indie premium vs service games?
  • How to protect IP and architectural solutions in the era of rapid AI content generation?

Key Points

  • AI has become ubiquitous: used in narrative, art, code, support, and analytics; time-to-market is shrinking to a quarter and below
  • Indie competitiveness is growing: small teams with AI deliver the workload of large studios; indie premium ROI often surpasses large multiplayer AAA experiments
  • Mobile leads (≈55% of revenue), and the service model with regular updates and events is key to retention and LTV
  • Metaverses and UGC/VR are strengthening: Fortnite has turned into a platform, Roblox/Minecraft/VRChat confirm the co-creation trend
  • Big players in practice: King generates levels with AI; Supercell uses AI matchmaking; Hearthstone speeds up card balance with AI
  • Tools: active discussion of Claude (Anthropic), Sora, Grok, Unity vs Unreal, Cursor IDE, GitHub Copilot; for vibe coding, Unity is more convenient
  • Organizational challenge: in large companies AI is slowed by legacy; the solution is small skunkworks units (strike teams) for quick pilots
  • Legal: the importance of patenting architectural ideas and building IP policies; AI’s legal framework is still lagging
  • Automation cases: auto-replies to 1,000+ emails/day with training on response styles and feedback to dev teams; personal cases of building a CRM in a week with token spend
  • Market and scale: by 2026—up to 4 billion players; in 2024, industry revenue around ≈$188 billion, half the population is the target market

Conclusions

  • AI radically increases productivity and reduces development costs, strengthening the position of indies and small teams.
  • Service models, UGC, and metaverses build sustainable revenue and communities around games.
  • Large studios need flexible AI units for pilots without risking legacy products; legal IP protection should keep pace with AI adoption.

Decisions Made

  • Create and/or expand small in-studio AI skunkworks teams for rapid pilots.
  • Launch pilots for automating support and handling player requests with feedback loop into development.
  • Introduce AI into GDD preparation and architectural design of prototypes.
  • Continue coordination and case sharing in the GCA Telegram group and prepare a follow-up session on AI tools.

Action Items

  • Set up subscriptions and launch sandboxes for key tools (Claude, Cursor, GitHub Copilot) for teams.
  • Choose 2–3 processes for rapid automation (support, level/content generation, matchmaking/balance) and set KPIs.
  • Develop an IP policy and patent strategy for architectural solutions and AI pipelines.
  • Audit stacks (Unity/Unreal) for vibe coding and plan AI integration into CI/CD.
  • Explore entry into the Chinese market (partnerships, competitions in Guangzhou/Hangzhou) and UGC platforms (Fortnite, Roblox).
