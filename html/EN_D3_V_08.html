Literacy and critical thinking in the digital age: AI in journalism and PR

Natalya Abramova — Senior Lecturer in AI at MNU, co-founder of Kazakhstan's first AI PR studio «Ozgeshe»
Oraz Arshat — SEO specialist at Joint Stock Company «Qazcontent»
Asel Kuzhakhmetova — Director of Communications and Marketing at Sergek Group, author of the Telegram channel AYEL
Zhiger Shudanbekov — PR manager of the fintech company «Бухта»

Annotation

The panel discussed how AI removes routine in PR and newsrooms (monitoring, analytics, reporting, video/content generation) but amplifies the risks of fakes, hallucinations, and the blurring of authorship. Speakers emphasized the shortage of local data and the importance of localization (Kazakh/Russian contexts), as well as the shift in search behavior from SEO to LLM answers. Consensus: AI is a tool and an assistant, and competitiveness will be defined by the skill of prompt engineering, fact-checking, and human expertise.

Goals

  • Show practical scenarios for integrating AI into PR and journalism to reduce costs and accelerate processes
  • Outline risks (fakes, hallucinations, cultural context) and ways to minimize them through standards and fact-checking
  • Discuss localization and the shortage of Kazakhstani data, and ways to increase it
  • Formulate a strategy to adapt to the shift from classic SEO to search in LLMs/chats
  • Identify key team skills: prompt engineering, analytics, storytelling

Key Questions

  • Will AI replace workers in media and PR, and which roles will remain human?
  • How will SEO evolve amid LLM search (AEO/Answer Engine Optimization), and what should companies do now?
  • How to ensure the quality and reliability of AI content given the local cultural context and language?
  • What standards and data are needed for sustainable localization (Kazakh/Russian) and to avoid hallucinations?
  • How quickly will AI catch up in quality in Kazakh and Russian, and how should teams prepare?

Key Points

  • AI removes routine in PR: bot monitoring of mentions, analysis of reach/sentiment, generation of reports and campaign plans
  • Modern generative video/images (Sora, Veo 3, Midjourney) sharply reduce production costs but require controllability and repeatability
  • The main risk is fakes and hallucinations: mandatory fact-checking and transparent labeling of AI content are needed
  • The shortage of local data in Kazakhstan hinders accurate generation and localization (ornaments, cultural codes, Kazakh language)
  • Search engines and platforms are strengthening E-E-A-T requirements (experience, expertise, authoritativeness, trust) — this is critical for newsrooms and brands
  • The shift of user search into LLMs requires moving from keywords to entities, structured data, and AEO
  • AI is an assistant, not an authority: human storytelling, analytics, and honesty build audience trust
  • The skill of prompt engineering and continuous learning of tools are becoming basic digital literacy
  • Automation saves resources, but without standards, processes, and governance the result is unstable
  • Internal communications and the actual quality of the product are more important than external PR — reputation is validated by people and communities

Conclusions

  • AI augments professionals by removing routine and speeding up production, but it requires strict fact-checking and human accountability.
  • For Kazakhstan, the key to quality is enriching local data and localization standards; without this, models will miss the context.
  • Promotion strategy is shifting toward answer engines and E-E-A-T; brands need to structure knowledge and manage the reputation of experts.

Decisions Made

  • Agreed to treat AI as a staff assistant for monitoring, analytics, and reporting, with mandatory human quality control.
  • Agreed to develop editorial standards for fact-checking and labeling of AI content.
  • Decided to launch pilots for generative video/visuals (Sora/Veo/Midjourney) for content tasks with ROI evaluation.
  • Agreed to build up local datasets: expanding Kazakhstan Wikipedia, publishing structured data and case studies.
  • Decided to adapt the SEO strategy for LLM/Answer Engine: focus on E-E-A-T, entities, schema/structured data, and Q&A content.

Action Items

  • Conduct an audit of current data and content for localization (Kazakh/Russian), structure and open key datasets.
  • Implement mention-monitoring bots and an auto-reporting pipeline; set up sentiment and reach analysis.
  • Develop and approve an AI governance policy: prompt rules, fact verification, labeling, and version storage.
  • Launch employee training on prompt engineering, E-E-A-T, and AEO (LLM search), with practice on real cases.
  • Pilot generative video/images for PR campaigns; define quality metrics, brand safety, and legal risks.
  • Update the content strategy: more expert columns with author/qualification attribution, FAQ and Q&A blocks for LLM indexing.
